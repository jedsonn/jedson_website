- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6765742
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

The insurance sector is experiencing accelerated digitalization driven by artificial intelligence, data analytics, cloud computing, and automation technologies. While conventional AI applications have enhanced operational efficiency, they remain predominantly task-oriented and dependent on human intervention. Agentic Artificial Intelligence (Agentic AI) represents an emerging paradigm that enables autonomous, goal-directed decision-making in complex insurance processes. This paper critically examines the adoption of agentic AI and digital technologies within the insurance industry, addressing managerial, organizational, and regulatory dimensions. Through systematic literature review, we identify key adoption drivers, barriers, and research gaps pertaining to governance, trust, workforce transformation, and regulatory compliance. We propose a managementoriented framework incorporating phased adoption strategies, robust governance mechanisms, and human-on-the-loop collaboration models to facilitate responsible implementation. The framework emphasizes transparency, accountability, and human supervision within highly regulated insurance environments. Our contributions provide practical guidance for insurance organizations seeking to balance operational efficiency with regulatory compliance and stakeholder trust in autonomous AI deployment.

- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6766026

### abstract

Process equipment lifecycle data include process, mechanical, manufacturing, quality, and in-service operation data. However, these data are distributed across heterogeneous sources and structures and are strongly constrained by engineering semantics and standards, creating persistent bottlenecks in data acquisition, organization, interpretation, and trustworthy governance. To address this issue, this study proposes an integrated method for data space architecture definition and AI-Agent closed-loop autonomous operation and maintenance for the process equipment industry. First, a formal architecture definition method for the process equipment data space is developed to address multi-source heterogeneity, strong semantic coupling, and complex dependencies in cross-system data flows. A unified representation framework is established, covering data object types, structured states, type mappings, object structuring, and permission mappings, thus providing a formal basis for object-oriented organization, semantic alignment, quality constraint enforcement, and trustworthy sharing of multi-source data. On this basis, an AI Agent based closed-loop operation and maintenance mechanism is introduced to support anomaly perception, reasoning, task execution, and experiential memory, thereby strengthening dynamic governance and continuous evolution of the data space. Based on multi-database data, 320 labeled anomalous samples were constructed. Experimental results show that the proposed method achieves 0.92 precision for field-level detection, 0.94 recall for combination-level detection, and 0.92 F1-score for consistency detection. Semantic classification accuracy reaches 93.3%, while Top-1 and Top-3 root-cause localization accuracies are 88% and 96%, respectively. These results demonstrate its engineering applicability for long-term autonomous operation and maintenance of process equipment data spaces.

### abstract

Background: Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) threatens planetary health. However, within the Agri–Food–Health Continuum (AFHC), the evolution of antibiotic resistance gene (ARG) risk, driven by non-antibiotic factors like climate and pesticides, remains poorly characterised across spatiotemporal scales. Methods: By integrating backpropagation neural network (BPNN) and convolutional neural network–long short-term memory (CNN–LSTM) architectures, we utilised metagenomic datasets from 47 habitats and 150 indicators to quantify driver contributions and project the ARG Risk Index (RI) across 30 countries to 2050. A Strategic Management Matrix (SMM) was developed to discern core drivers within agricultural paradigms, including the USA, the UK, and China. Interpretation: Global AMR governance should shift from linear antibiotic-reduction towards fine-tuning of systemic structures. - affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6725982
- keyword hits: generative ai, prompting

### abstract

This article presents a pedagogical framework that leverages computational logic and Generative AI (GenAI) to support instructional design and educational decision-making in higher education. By drawing on core programming constructs such as if/else, while, define, and assert, the framework reimagines prompt-based interaction as a structured and iterative reasoning process in which learners externalize thought, manage uncertainty, and refine understanding, transforming prompts into cognitive tools for learning. These structured interactions generate a new layer of actionable data, prompt iterations, logical scaffolds, and validation checkpoints, termed epistemic learning analytics. Rather than focusing on output correctness, this approach prioritizes reasoning strategies, enabling targeted feedback and curriculum refinement. An exploratory design-based study with 40 undergraduate engineering students provides initial indications that structured prompting may support epistemic agency and emergent reflective control, even among GenAI novices. Through mixed-method analysis and unsupervised clustering (PCA + KMeans), we observe preliminary patterns consistent with three learner profiles, procedural users, emerging strategists, and epistemic orchestrators, each reflecting distinct patterns of logic use and cognitive engagement. By positioning GenAI as both a reasoning support and a source of learning analytics, this framework offers an accessible and transferable model for integrating AI into instructional practice.

- title: A Conceptual Systems Governance Framework for Autonomous Financial Execution: The Sovereign Payment Agent Model
- authors: Avik Nandi
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-19
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6765180
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

The deployment of autonomous AI agents in financial infrastructure introduces governance challenges that existing regulatory and institutional frameworks were not designed to address. As AI-native payment systems become capable of initiating, routing, and settling financial transactions without human activation, traditional assumptions underlying payment oversight — including deterministic auditability, procedural policy enforcement, and human accountability at the point of execution — become increasingly insufficient. This paper proposes the Sovereign Payment Agent (SPA) model, a conceptual systems governance framework intended to support the controlled deployment of autonomous payment agents within enterprise and regulated financial environments. The framework synthesizes principles from treasury mandate governance, payment authorization hierarchies, information security architecture, AI governance literature, and model risk management guidance to establish a structured governance envelope for autonomous execution. The paper introduces four primary contributions: (1) a Governance Envelope architecture incorporating Policy-as-Code constraints and audit artifact generation; (2) a five-tier Agent Autonomy Classification (AAC) model enabling calibrated deployment of autonomous execution capabilities; (3) a structured audit artifact standard supporting explainability and regulatory examination; and (4) an adversarial risk architecture addressing prompt injection, signal manipulation, policy drift, and model extraction threats specific to AI-native payment systems. This study adopts a conceptual systems-design methodology and does not present empirical validation or production implementation results. Rather, it aims to contribute a governance-oriented conceptual framework for future research, institutional experimentation, and regulatory engagement concerning autonomous financial execution.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6786041`

- title: An ECG Foundation Model for Generalizable Cardiac Function Prediction across the Lifespan
- authors: Yuting Yang, Lorenzo Peracchio, Joshua Mayourian, Timothy Miller, William  G. La Cava
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-19
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6786041
- keyword hits: fine-tuning, foundation model

### abstract

Background: Artificial intelligence-enhanced electrocardiography (AI-ECG) enables scalable, low-cost cardiac dysfunction screening, but existing models are annotation-intensive and predominantly adult-derived, leaving paediatric generalizability uncertain. Paediatric cohorts exhibit highly variable cardiac morphology and function compared to adults, which may be useful for learning generalizable AI-ECG models. Methods: We pretrained ECG-Fyler on a predominantly paediatric, all-age cohort at Boston Children’s Hospital (1992--2023), annotated with a cardiology-specific coding system (Fyler codes), and evaluated it on assessments from echocardiography (echo) and cardiac magnetic resonance (CMR) studies. We validated on an external adult cohort from Columbia University Irving Medical Center. Performance was benchmarked against several AI-ECG foundation models by AUROC across age groups, lesion types, and limited-data scenarios. Findings: The pretraining cohort comprised 782,138 ECGs from 255,271 patients (median age: 10.9 years, IQR: [2.8--16.8]). Internal evaluation included 178,495 ECG-echo pairs (median age: 10.9 [3.7--17.0]) and 8,584 ECG-CMR pairs (median age: 20.7 [15.6--29.6]). External validation included 82,543 ECG-echo pairs from adults (median age: 64.0 [52.0--74.0]). ECG-Fyler improved AUROC compared to other approaches across biventricular dysfunction and dilation tasks, with the largest gains in low-data settings. In internal validation, ECG-Fyler detected low left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF ≤ 40%) from only 100 fine-tuning samples (AUROC: 0.80, 95% CI: [0.78--0.80]), outperforming other models (AUROC < 0.65) and improving with additional fine-tuning (AUROC: 0.94 [0.93--0.94]). Similar improvements were observed for CMR-derived LVEF, RVEF, and ventricular dilation. In external validation on adults, ECG-Fyler exhibited an AUROC of 0.83 (CI: [0.82--0.85]) for LVEF ≤ 40%. After fine-tuning on less than 10% of external data, LVEF ≤ 45% performance (AUROC: 0.87 [0.86--0.88]) outperformed a fully trained, site-specific prior model (AUROC: 0.85 [0.84--0.87]). Interpretation: Pretraining on richly annotated, paediatric-dominant ECGs yields models that transfer efficiently across institutions and ages, supporting AI-ECG screening and triage when labels or imaging access are limited.

- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6802902

### abstract

​While existing backdoor attacks achieve malicious alignment through fine-tuning, their reliance on extensive parameter updates renders them computationally prohibitive as model sizes continue to scale.To mitigate the aforementioned issue, we propose CMCAttack, a novel attack framework that investigates the vulnerability of VLMs to backdoor attacks by Cross-Modal Conflicts through malicious instructions and poisoned demonstrations, without requiring access to the model architecture. Specifically, CMCAttack incorporates three attack strategies including object conflict, attribute conflict, and relation conflict, respectively.By introducing semantic inconsistencies between the visual and textual modalities in the demonstrations, we construct a versatile suite of unimodal and dual-modality triggers, and further enhance attack effectiveness through malicious instructions.Unlike existing backdoor attacks against VLMs, our method induces the model to learn concealed malicious associations from cross-modal conflicts without relying on fine-tuning. Extensive experiments on VLMs, spanning parameter scales from 8B to 235B, demonstrate the effectiveness of our method. In particular, on the Qwen3-VL series, our attack achieves an average success rate of 99.7% across distinct conflict categories.
