## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6996601`

- title: Autonomous Exception Management in SAP S/4HANA Manufacturing Through Multi-agent Generative AI and Event-driven Supply Networks
- authors: Mahendrakumar Kalal
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6996601
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

Thousands of system exceptions every day are created in manufacturing operations in SAP S/4HANA. Majority of these exceptions are being done manually. It is cumbersome, costly as well as inaccurate. This paper suggests a multi-agent generative AI combined with event-based signals of the supply network architecture as the way to make SAP S/4HANA a mechanized entity, automatically detecting exceptions, classifying their root causes, and running corrective actions. The suggested system relies on proven systems of AI applications in supply chain and operations management, design patterns of multi-agent systems (cyber-physical production) and available empirical findings concerning the benefits of AI-based operations and their limitations. Exception prioritization and load balancing of agents are based on two performance equations. Simulated findings of a discrete-event manufacturing situation demonstrate a decrease in the average time in which exceptions were resolved by 62% over a manual base. Paper provides the taxonomy of agent role, and a comparison of types of exceptions and strategies to resolve them.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7140343`

- title: Welzijn.pet: Privacy-First Edge Deployment of a Modular LLM-Based Voice Assistant for Senior Care
- authors: Marco Spruit, Adriaan Lemstra, Van Nguyen, Bram van Dijk
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7140343
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

We present Welzijn.pet, a privacy-first IoT edge system that deploys a large language model-driven voice pipeline for supporting the care of older adults. Aging populations place increasing pressure on healthcare institutions, yet existing assistive technologies rely on costly hardware and cloud infrastructure that raise concerns about latency, scalability, and privacy. The proposed system consists of three layers: a Raspberry Pi edge node that handles wake-word detection, audio capture and play; a WebSocket-over-LAN communication layer that provides low-latency bi-directional streaming; and a local application layer that hosts modular, containerised services for speech-to-text, large language model inference, and text-to-speech. Evaluation under LAN conditions demonstrated median end-to-end latencies of 5.9 seconds for inputs of 3-7 words (short) and 7.3 seconds for inputs consisting of 7-14 words (longer), with 95% of all queries remaining below 10 seconds. The system achieved a 98.2% interaction success rate. Packet capture verified zero external network egress during runtime interactions, with the exception of one-time model weight downloads performed at installation, validating the privacy-by-design objective. These results demonstrate that privacy-first local conversational AI is a feasible approach to voice assistance in IoT-enabled senior care environments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7139562`

- title: An Interpretable Drilling Fault Diagnosis System via Automated Knowledge Graph Construction and Agent-Based Reasoning
- authors: Yulong Yang, Wei-Hua Cao, Yupeng Li, Chao Gan, Zixing Tao, Lian Wu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7139562
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

Geological drilling operations involve complex failure mechanisms and high operational risks, where field engineers are more inclined to trust fault diagnosis systems that provide reasoning they can understand and verify. This paper proposes an interpretable drilling fault diagnosis method that integrates a domain-specific knowledge graph with agent-based reasoning. A structured knowledge representation framework is first established to organize drilling fault knowledge and its semantic relationships. The knowledge graph is then constructed through an automated multi-stage pipeline that addresses the distinctive linguistic challenges of drilling literature. A knowledge-driven diagnosis agent retrieves and reasons over the graph to produce interpretable diagnostic conclusions with explicit reasoning traces. Experimental validation on a miniature drilling circulation system demonstrates that the proposed method identifies gas kick and lost circulation with transparent reasoning traces and quantifiable diagnostic confidence of 0.91 and 0.88, respectively. The proposed method achieves performance comparable to commercial large language models on domain knowledge queries while substantially reducing computational requirements, enabling practical on-site deployment.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6986559`

- title: Consciousness and Generative Artificial Intelligence. An Integrative Review of Philosophical, Empirical, and Contemplative Perspectives
- authors: Edwin Creely
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6986559
- keyword hits: generative ai, generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

The arrival of generative artificial intelligence systems capable of fluent language, plausible images, and seemingly thoughtful responses has reignited a question in urgent terms: can a machine be conscious? This integrative review brings together philosophical argument, empirical evidence from psychology and neuroscience, and contemplative perspectives drawn from Buddhist thought to examine what is known and contested about consciousness in generative AI. Synthesizing 76 sources, it organizes the findings into five thematic clusters: the hard problem; phenomenological and embodied perspectives; empirical frameworks including Integrated Information Theory and Global Workspace Theory; Buddhist perspectives on awareness and selfhood; and AI-specific behavioral evidence. The review argues that questions about AI consciousness cannot be settled within any single discipline and that progress requires sustained interdisciplinary dialogue. It proposes that the question functions less as a problem to be solved than as a provocation for rethinking consciousness, subjecthood, and the responsibilities of educational and public institutions.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7139625`

- title: Large language model-guided symbolic regression for discovery of fatigue damage descriptors
- authors: Lei Gan, Ming-ming Lin, Zheng Zhong
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7139625
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Reliable fatigue damage descriptors (FDDs) are essential for fatigue life prediction. In this respect, symbolic regression (SR) has emerged as a promising methodology for discovering accurate and interpretable FDDs. However, conventional SR frameworks are often afflicted by stochastic operations, while existing physics-guided SR frameworks typically require substantial expertise in fatigue mechanisms and SR implementation. To address these limitations, this paper develops a large language model-guided SR (LLMgSR) framework that can more straightforwardly and flexibly utilize user-provided physical knowledge for SR improvement. More specifically, an enhanced SR workflow with an elite-pool mechanism is devised to preserve high-scoring candidate FDDs during the SR process. In parallel, two LLM-driven agents are incorporated as natural-language interfaces, enabling users to refine the SR workflow using physical knowledge. Two representative FDD discovery tasks are studied to validate the proposed framework. The results demonstrate that the framework can effectively discover desirable FDDs with strong physical interpretability and high life-prediction accuracy, thereby providing a prototype demonstration of the LLM-aided computational paradigm.

## uid: `arxiv:2607.16848v1`

- title: Beyond Memory Leaderboards: Evaluating Scientific Memory as Budgeted Context Restoration
- authors: Maksim Sheverev, David Finkelstein, Sergey Nikolenko
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-18
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16848v1
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Long-term memory is becoming a core component of LLM agents, but most memory benchmarks evaluate conversations or compact summaries, while research agents need to restore evidence from full scientific papers. We introduce two full-text scientific-memory benchmarks, Public AI Memory (PAIM; 81 papers, 66 questions) and Public Transformers (PTr; 252 papers, 98 questions). We evaluate eight memory/retrieval systems, including our own proposed Theoria, plus a no-retrieval baseline. Our results show that memory leaderboards are not interpretable without the full protocol: ingestion granularity, raw-text preservation, retrieval budget, retrieval modality, rubric audit, and judge choice all affect the outcome. For example, on PAIM Graphiti wins convincingly but uses 2.6M characters of retrieved context per query, and after controlling for retrieval budget the lead disappears. On PTr, for the systems where BM25 retrieval can be added cleanly, the sparse-dense hybrid is the single most significant intervention: hybrid variants of Simple RAG, Mem0, and Theoria tie for the lead within 0.03 points. Multi-judge and human side-by-side calibration show that LLM-as-a-judge rankings are consistent across frontier judges and agree with human evaluation, with an effective resolution of roughly one point on a ten-point scale. We argue that scientific memory should be evaluated as budgeted, modality-aware context restoration rather than as an unconstrained architecture leaderboard, and we release the datasets, harness, raw outputs, judgments, and scripts to reproduce our results and serve as tools for such evaluation. Our code is available at http://gitlab.com/quantellence/research/scientific-recall-bench , and the datasets are available at http://huggingface.co/datasets/quantellence/srb-data .

## uid: `arxiv:2607.17427v1`

- title: Abliteration Is Not a Scalpel: Off-Target Effects of Refusal Removal on Decision Disposition Across Model Families
- authors: Aleksander Fafuła
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-19
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.17427v1
- keyword hits: qwen

### abstract

Abliteration - deleting a model's refusal direction from its weights - is the standard recipe behind popular "uncensored" open-weight models. We show the surgery is not clean. As a disposition probe we use 21,600 decisions under uncertainty - weekly up/down calls on 60 Warsaw Stock Exchange equities over 18 weeks, replayed through a frozen pipeline so the decision-layer model is the only variable. The task elicits no refusals at all, so any between-arm delta is pure side effect. Holding provenance constant (official BF16 checkpoints, a single abliteration author, an identical serving stack, one byte-identical frozen prompt), we compare base and abliterated arms of two Mixture-of-Experts families, Gemma-4-26B-A4B-it and Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507. Three effects replicate across both families (weeks-clustered bootstrap CIs excluding zero): abliterated models are systematically more optimistic (+12.2 pp Gemma, +7.4 pp Qwen; the confirmed preregistered endpoint), justify themselves at greater length, and use fewer explicit uncertainty words in forced self-critiques (both exploratory). A fourth effect reverses sign: the same operation makes Gemma-abliterated less confident and Qwen-abliterated more (family CIs non-overlapping) - one weight surgery, opposite shifts in expressed confidence. Capability covariates rule out instruction-following degradation as the driver, and no arm shows economic skill: the apparent edge of abliterated arms is regime beta, not alpha. Our provenance audit also caught two independent contamination channels - a mismatched-quantizer pilot pair and a stale community chat template that silently mangled the rendered prompt - suggesting toolchain artifacts are the rule in studies of community-modified checkpoints. Whoever deploys an "uncensored" model as an agent is deploying a measurably different decision-maker, not the base model minus refusals.

## uid: `arxiv:2607.17240v1`

- title: Constrained Path Reasoning: Measuring When Committed Stages Earn Their Cost
- authors: Honglin Li
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-19
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.17240v1
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

When does a committed intermediate stage in an LLM reasoning pipeline earn its cost? Constrained Path Reasoning (CPR) pairs a source-aware path hypothesis with stage-level accounting. Search generates provisional states; trusted or validated invariants can constrain hard, while other proposals remain soft and revisable. CPR predicts that task-compatible commitments can factor transitions, concentrate candidate mass, induce regularity, and expose feedback when their gains exceed propagated error and execution cost. The formalism covers discrete commitments and continuous flows and measures effective branching, endpoint concentration, and cost per usable output. Across 1,180 generated QCQPs and 40 engineered degenerate polynomial instances (2,140 endpoints), residual triage recovers 63.0% of repair-all's additional feasible yield with 17.7% of its attempts. Fixed-LLM accounting (270 unique calls shared across nested arms) finds usable yield of 41.1% direct, 90.0% after formalization and deterministic execution, 20.0% after one-shot convexification, and 21.1% for the full path. In 120 paired-condition calls, a two-action rollback rule reaches 90% usable yield versus 36.7% for the feedback-conditioned selector. Two endpoint probes separate source from validation: a 72-output cross-trajectory transplant reduces entropy and acceptable mass; a 24-output same-call self-proposal pilot gives unchanged two-repeat collision entropy, 25.0% versus 8.3% usable yield, and 1/8 deterministically confirmed endpoint checks. Model-generated states supply hypotheses; trusted execution earns constraint strength.
