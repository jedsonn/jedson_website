- title: ASG-LLM: An LLM-Based Pipeline for Automated Scraping Code Generation in Security-Sensitive Environments
- authors: Sokheang Chan, Naro Vorn, Vungsovanreach Kong, Chang Kue Seok, Jong-Hyun Kim, Dr. Anand Nayyar, Tae-Kyung KIM
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-09
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6904184
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Automating interactions with security-sensitive web services remains difficult due to dynamic frontends, session-bound tokens, and undocumented parameter-generation logic. Traditionally, developers must manually analyze captured traffic, hypothesize parameter-generation routines, and iteratively test internally defined functions until the interaction succeeds. We propose ASG-LLM, an execution grounded pipeline that generates scraping modules from a single successful browser session trace. ASG-LLM captures full HTTP(S) traffic, removes irrelevant requests through multi-stage filtering, and constructs a dependency-aware representation of the core interaction flow. When security-critical parameters cannot be reconstructed from the trace alone, the FUNC Retriever identifies a small set of reusable internal function descriptions using HyDE-guided selective retrieval, enabling the LLM to select compatible parameter-generation routines based on behavioral similarity rather than keyword matching, without exposing proprietary implementation code. Generated modules are executed in a controlled environment and iteratively repaired using structured failure feedback. ASG-LLM was evaluated on representative banking and public-sector portals. In our case studies, ASG-LLM reduces developer effort by cutting traffic analysis and basic code implementation from hours to minutes and reducing security-handling effort from a full-day task to a couple of hours. At the model level, HyDE-based selective retrieval improves internal function recommendation accuracy from 30% to 70% Top-1 and from 60% to 90% Top-3 compared with a full-context baseline. These results suggest that combining trace grounding, selective retrieval, and execution-time validation is a promising direction for robust web automation in security-constrained environments.

### abstract

Recent advances in error-corrected qubits have accelerated the timeline for practical quantum computing. It poses a threat to cryptographic primitives used to secure financial systems, government infrastructure, communication networks, and DeFi (Decentralized Finance) ecosystems. This paper introduces a post-quantum secure federated DeFi framework that enables inter-bank collaboration to improve the inclusivity of individuals underserved by local lenders due to limited financial histories. Multiple banks contribute encrypted information batches to a virtual server, where lattice-based Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) enables end-to-end homomorphic computation. The server fuses local data-driven probabilistic assessments, expert beliefs, and verifiable evidence generated by the NASA-IBM Prithvi Geospatial Foundation Model (GFM), in encrypted format. Decentralized technologies are employed to ensure tamper-proof evidence and auditable accountability for all encrypted data exchanges between institutions and the server. The framework is tested on agricultural lending decisions for rural borrowers in Virginia.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6914530`

- title: Answering Without Informing: Evasive Disclosure and Analyst Information Production
- authors: Xuejia Xue, Shengmiao Bi
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-10
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6914530
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Mandatory investor-interaction platforms make corporate silence more costly, but they do not guarantee informative replies. Existing platform studies often measure whether firms respond or how closely answers resemble questions. Such measures do not directly show whether a reply resolves an investor's explicit information demand. Using Chinese exchange-operated question-response archives from 2010-2024, the study constructs a pre-forecast measure of answer completeness based on LLM classification and human validation. It finds that greater pre-forecast response evasiveness is associated with larger analyst forecast errors. A one-standard-deviation higher evasiveness measure corresponds to a baseline forecast-error measure about 3.4% of its sample mean higher. The relation is stronger when analysts have fewer substitutes for weak public answers, including lower reputation status, weaker brokerage resources, and greater geographic distance from covered firms. Potential-channel tests show that firms evading investor questions reduce analysts' forecast-revision frequency for those firms and increase investors' search activity on other platforms.

- title: NetHarden: Auditable Attack-Graph Hardening with Multi-Metric and LLM-Reliability Evaluation
- authors: Justice Agyemang, Kwame  Agyeman-Prempeh Agyekum, Francisca Adoma Acheampong, Jerry  John Kponyo, Michael Agyare, Kwame Agyekum
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-10
- source: SSRN

### abstract

Attack-graph-based network hardening evaluations seldom ask whether the reported metric is the right metric or whether the increasingly common LLM reporting layer is reliable. \textsc{NetHarden} pairs a deterministic budgeted coverage-to-cost optimizer over host--vulnerability attack graphs (25 verified real CVEs from NVD) with a structured LLM remediation interface. The optimizer reaches 99.5\% mean weighted risk reduction on 30 synthetic networks and ties an LP-optimal set-cover ILP on all 130 networks (30-net plus 100-net expansion). Beyond the optimizer, a $\pm 50\%$ coefficient sensitivity sweep yields a 0.012\,pp spread, and two metric-independent attacker models (passive random-walk and adaptive Viterbi-style, the latter plan-aware) find all methods statistically indistinguishable from doing nothing, with the adaptive attacker's best-path probability identical to three decimals across seven methods on both suites. A six-model dual-judge LLM study with a 3-format prompt ablation finds every model returns a non-empty report on every plan; programmatic CVE- and host-level checks score 0.99--1.00 F1 with zero fabricated identifiers, while LLM-judge faithfulness reaches 6.21/10 with strong ordinal agreement ($\rho{=}0.875$) but zero agreement on the deployment threshold ($\kappa{=}0$). The combined evidence reframes \textsc{NetHarden} as a methodology contribution arguing for harder benchmarks and multi-objective evaluation, not a new optimizer.

Selection mechanisms play a crucial role in the performance of multi-objective evolutionary algorithms (MOEAs) as they guide the search process toward optimal solutions. Traditionally, MOEAs have been classified into: Pareto-based, indicator-based, and decomposition-based approaches. - title: Generative Artificial Intelligence and Product Innovation Performance: The Roles of Absorptive Capacity, Strategic Agility, and Intelligent Organizational Learning
- authors: Mahdi Sharaf Tadavani, Ali Pirzad
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-10
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6911303
- keyword hits: generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

This study investigates how industrial firms transform generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) into product innovation. Applying a capability-based perspective, we surveyed 400 managers from Iranian industrial firms and used structural equation modeling to test our model. Results demonstrate that GenAI significantly strengthens absorptive capacity. We validate a sequential mediation chain: GenAI → Absorptive Capacity → Intelligent Organizational Learning → Product Innovation Performance. While strategic agility and customer value co-creation are outcomes of absorptive capacity, only intelligent organizational learning directly drives product innovation. These findings emphasize that converting AI potential into innovation performance requires embedding knowledge into structured organizational learning mechanisms.
