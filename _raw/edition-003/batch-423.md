- title: Disrupting the Undisruptable: Agentic AI Innovations in Construction
- authors: Anson Tsz Kwan Chan, Erika Parn
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-23
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6933040
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Agentic artificial intelligence (AI) refers to systems capable of semi-autonomous planning to achieve pre-defined goals. It is reshaping innovation processes across industries; yet socially impactful industries such as construction, healthcare, and education remain resistant to technological changes. Grounded in disruptive innovation theory (DIT), this study explores how agentic AI challenges and reshapes innovation dynamics in the construction industry: a sector characterised by slow technological adoption, complex institutional constraints, and fragmented project structures. A qualitative approach is employed to conduct semi-structured interviews with 34 construction professionals across seven regions. Together with five categories of archival data, our findings reveal three dimensions: (1) triggers of innovation, comprising institutional pressure, project delivery crises and data fragmentation; (2) resistance to disruption, comprising institutional professional legitimacy and project liability; and (3) institution-enabled innovation, comprising agent governance, risk allocation and knowledge embeddedness. The results show that the construction industry occupies a contested space between sustaining and disruptive innovation trajectories. Heavily regulated, socially impactful, and liability-intensive industries are both reliant and constrained by institutional mediators to shape their pace of technological disruption. This study introduces the concept of institution-enabled innovation as the third pathway beyond existing DIT emphasis on either market competition or technological performance. It contributes by empirically extending DIT application in a sector-specific analysis, while highlighting its societal implications as shaped by institutions. Practical recommendations are offered for construction practitioners and policymakers seeking to navigate advanced AI adoption in highly institutionalised settings.
