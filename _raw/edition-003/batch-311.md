- title: Agentic Commerce Governance Framework™: Classifying Enterprise Liability Exposure in the Age of Machine Buyers
- authors: Frank Meltke
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6849539
- keyword hits: agentic, ai agent

### abstract

The emergence of autonomous AI agents as active participants in commercial transactions represents the most significant structural shift in enterprise architecture since the advent of e-commerce. This paper introduces the Agentic Commerce Governance Framework™, a six-level classification system (Level 0 through Level 5) that maps the progressive elimination of human psychological friction from the purchase transaction. The framework identifies Level 3 (Delegated Execution) as the threshold at which traditional risk governance models first break down, and defines three structural governance failures that create liability exposure at Levels 3 through 5: liability without visibility, the breakdown of psychological friction as a control mechanism, and data maturity deficits that transform product catalogs into arbitrage vectors. The paper introduces a dual governance architecture combining the Mandate Protocol for top-down agent authorization with tactical exposure diagnostics for bottom-up vulnerability identification as a framework for establishing comprehensive organizational governance posture in advance of agentic commerce deployment.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6949226`

- title: AI Listening to Central Banks: From Words to Markets and Macroeconomic Outcomes
- authors: Antoaneta Amza, Rahul Tak, Caliman Stefan-Daniel, Daniel Traian Pele
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6949226
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

​Central banks play a crucial role in maintaining economic stability and ensuring the proper functioning of financial markets. Their communication can significantly influence the economy by shaping market participants’ perceptions and expectations. While most existing research on this topic focuses on the U.S. Federal Reserve or the European Central Bank, this paper provides a novel perspective by applying large language models to analyze the minutes of monetary policy meetings from Central and Eastern European central banks and their impact on key macroeconomic and financial indicators. The results show that, even for these economies, the informational content embedded in official communication has explanatory power, offering valuable insights for a region that remains relatively underexplored in the literature. The impact is particularly evident on the interbank money market, while the responses of other market segments are mixed, reflecting the predominantly bank-based structure of these financial systems. Furthermore, the analysis conducted through a Bayesian structural VAR model highlights a close link between central bank communication and macroeconomic developments in the studied economies. In particular, communication appears to be interpreted in a dual manner, reflecting both expectations of future monetary policy actions and information about underlying economic conditions. By documenting the relevance of communication shocks in Central and Eastern Europe, this paper extends the literature on monetary policy transmission and emphasizes the importance of considering communication as a complementary tool to conventional policy instruments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6951049`

- title: Talk-to-Train: LLM-Guided Successor-Feature Reinforcement Learning for Reconfigurable Multi-Objective Train Operation
- authors: Yiding Yu, Pengfei Sun, Cong Wang, Wenteng Xu, Zheng Liu, Qingyuan Wang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6951049
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Long-distance train operation optimization is challenged by sustained decision horizons, strong coupling among multiple objectives, and frequent changes in operational requirements. Conventional multi-objective optimization methods often rely on complex model formulation and predefined weight settings, which limits their applicability and makes it difficult to adapt to different operational preferences. To address these issues, this paper proposes FlexiMORL (Flexible Multi-Objective Reinforcement Learning), a multi-objective reinforcement learning method for long-horizon train control. FlexiMORL introduces a large language model to guide candidate action sampling. It also combines extended successor features with a preference-conditioned vector value representation. This design enables value recomposition under different preferences and supports reconfigurable train operation optimization. Experimental results show that it can flexibly generate control policies with different operational priorities by adjusting external preference weights, without retraining the model. The proposed method also demonstrates favorable performance in policy reconfiguration, training stability, and task adaptability.

- title: How multimodal social media and participatory on-site surveys reveal public perception of riverfront linear public space
- authors: Chenghao Yang, KAIYUE MA, Jiali He, Tongtong Liu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-16
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6951951
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Understanding public perception is essential for guiding the spatial optimization and renewal of linear riverfront public spaces. Although social media provides an efficient source of user-generated content reflecting public feedback and visual focal points, its inherent biases remain under scrutiny. This study proposes a multimodal framework that integrates deep learning-based analysis of multimodal social media data with participatory on-site surveys, taking the linear public space Haihe River in Tianjin, China, as a case study. The framework comprises three components: multimodal social media analysis, statistical analysis of on-site surveys, and a comparison analysis. For text data, BERTopic was employed to identify dominant themes, followed by re-clustering through Large Language Model (LLM), while a RoBERTa-based detected seven emotional states. For visual data, a MobileNet-v2 day-night classifier was combined with ResNet-50 for image classification, Mask2Former for panoptic segmentation, and Place Pulse 2.0 for human perception evaluation. Participatory on-site surveys incorporated structured observations, questionnaire, and Photovoice. Triangulation across three dimensions of place cognition, emotional experience, and visual preference comprehensively examined public perception. Results show that the social media text predominantly onto Place Identity (69.1%) and unsupervised five clusters, with a neutral 39.8% and joy 49.6% emotion, whereas the on-site streams concentrated on Riverside Routines and were dominated by joy. Visual preferences converged across modalities, with SkyVI, BVI and WVI consistently foregrounded. The study offers a practical and generalisable methodology for assessing public perception in riverfront linear public spaces, and provides urban designers with evidence to foster placemaking and place attachment.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6956765`

- title: Democratic Governance and Substantive Equality in Generative Artificial Intelligence Public Policies: Latin American Governments
- authors: Juliana Vivar Vera, Elsa Catalina Olivas Castellanos
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-17
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6956765
- keyword hits: generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

Substantive equality is one of the main challenges of public policies in Latin America, especially in structural inequality contexts and digital gaps. Concurrently, the expansion of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) is transforming public governance, digital communication and State-citizenship interaction. This paper analyzes the relationship between participatory governance and substantive equality through a comparative study of the national strategies of Brazil, Chile and Uruguay. This qualitative research is based on digital governance frameworks, public policies, and institutional initiatives related to AI. The study focuses on the mechanisms of participation in the formulation processes of IAGen. The paper sustains that GenAI does not inherently promote substantive equality; rather, its impact depends on the governance structures in which it is implemented. The findings indicate that the three countries have incorporated mechanisms for multi-stakeholder participation in the governance of the IAGen. However, limitations persist in terms of representation and effective participation. Decision-making processes are temporary and dominated by the government, business, and academic sectors, while historically disadvantaged groups remain underrepresented. This indicates a preference for technical legitimacy over democratic legitimacy. The study identifies substantive equality as a necessary category in IAGen governance. It concludes with a proposal for a governance model of the IAGen for Latin America based on the democratic co-production of public policies and supported by pillars and criteria of substantive equality and digital sovereignty.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6955442`

- title: Agentic AI Orchestration Capability: A Dynamic Capabilities Framework for Strategic Innovation in the Age of Autonomous Agents
- authors: Albert Adusei Brobbey, Narayan Bhosale
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-17
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6955442
- keyword hits: agentic, ai agent

### abstract

Autonomous AI agents are shifting artificial intelligence from decision support to autonomous workflow execution. Strategy research has examined AI adoption, digital transformation, and AI-enabled business models, but it has not yet explained why some firms convert autonomous agents into innovation advantage while others accumulate automation risk. This conceptual article develops the construct of agentic AI orchestration capability: a firm's ability to configure, coordinate, govern, learn from, and strategically redeploy autonomous AI agents across innovation workflows. Drawing on dynamic capabilities, resource orchestration, and digital innovation research, the article identifies five microfoundations: strategic agent portfolio sensing, modular agent deployment, memory and knowledge governance, human-agent escalation, and risk-contained experimentation. The framework explains how these routines influence innovation speed, strategic agility, business model renewal, and resilience. The article contributes by moving AI strategy research beyond adoption and augmentation toward capability formation in organizations where software agents increasingly perform economically significant work.
