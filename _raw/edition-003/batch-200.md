- title: A Methodological Protocol for the Generation and Evaluation of AI-Generated Cultural Heritage Content
- authors: Alejandro Galindo Dur&aacute;n, Cristina Prego de Oliver-Lopez, Cesar Bernal-Bravo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6759649
- keyword hits: generative artificial intelligence, prompting

### abstract

The growing use of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) in cultural heritage dissemination has outpaced the development of rigorous procedures to ensure historical accuracy, formal coherence, and educational reliability. This article proposes a methodological protocol for the generation and evaluation of AI-generated cultural heritage content, with a particular focus on image-based representations. To establish the need for such a protocol, a bibliometric review of literature published between 2020 and 2025 in Web of Science and Scopus was conducted, confirming the absence of a comprehensive and replicable framework specifically designed to assess the historical veracity of AI-generated heritage imagery.The proposed protocol integrates four components: (1) a standardised image-generation procedure based on zero-shot prompting and first-output selection; (2) an interdisciplinary expert evaluation instrument comprising 12 items organised into five analytical dimensions; (3) an automated multimodal validation layer designed to contrast human and AI-based assessments; and (4) a predefined analytical plan encompassing internal consistency, inter-rater agreement, descriptive statistics, and qualitative thematic analysis. Rather than presenting empirical findings, the article offers a reproducible framework intended to support future comparative studies and professional applications in heritage communication, education, and digital dissemination.The protocol aims to reduce the risks of misinformation, enhance methodological transparency, and promote historically grounded uses of GenAI in cultural heritage contexts.

Three principal frameworks anchor contemporary AI governance practice: ISO/IEC 42001:2023, the international AI management system standard; the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, the United States voluntary risk function framework with its July 2024 Generative AI Profile; and the EU AI Act, the world's first comprehensive risk-based AI regulation, with its November 2025 Digital Omnibus proposal currently subject to political negotiation. Third, the GCC AI governance landscape is converging toward ISO/IEC 42001 as the de facto international standard while developing region-specific instruments that complement rather than replicate it. Implications. Practitioners deploying AI in multi-jurisdictional contexts require integrated governance programmes that map controls to multiple frameworks simultaneously. ## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6778930`

- title: A physics-informed residual correction framework for pretrained tabular foundation model based battery health prognostics
- authors: Zhiqiang Li, Liu Teng, Wei Li, Weiwei Huo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-17
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6778930
- keyword hits: foundation model, in-context learning

### abstract

Accurate prediction of battery state of health (SOH) and remaining useful life (RUL) is essential for the safe and economical operation of lithium-ion battery systems, yet remains challenging due to complex degradation mechanisms and limited training data. This paper proposes a physics-informed residual correction framework that integrates a pretrained tabular foundation model with physics-informed neural networks for battery health prognostics. The framework operates through three hierarchical layers: a TabPFN prior layer that generates strong initial predictions via in-context learning, a lightweight correction head with zero-initialized output layer that learns the residual between the prior and the true target while preserving the pretrained prior at the start of training, and a multi-granularity physics-informed constraint layer offering a continuous spectrum from purely data-driven to strongly physics-constrained modeling. The framework requires only partial charging data from the 3.5 V to 4.0 V voltage window, generating 12 input features suitable for battery management system deployment. Experiments on three publicly available datasets (CALCE, MICH, SNL_NMC) demonstrate that the proposed TabPFN-DeepHPM achieves the best overall SOH R2 of 0.917 and RUL R2 of 0.873, with SOH RMSE of 0.020 and RUL RMSE of 42.6 cycles. The PINN-only baseline without TabPFN prior yields substantially lower RUL R2 of 0.815, confirming the critical importance of the pretrained prior. Ablation studies reveal that the zero initialization preserves the TabPFN prior at training onset, the 256-neuron correction head achieves Pareto optimality in the accuracy-efficiency tradeoff, and the residual corrections are bidirectional with no systematic bias (SOH correction standard deviation below 0.030 across all datasets).

- title: A Comparative Study between Hybrid ML Models and Few-Shot In-Context LLM Models for Detecting Non-Actionable Email in a Customer-Service Setting
- authors: Budsadee Sareerasart, Ekarat Rattagan
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-17
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6778885
- keyword hits: gpt-4, llm, prompt engineering

### abstract

In customer service, non-actionable emails—such as auto-replies, acknowledgments, and system notifications are also considered spam because they disrupt agent workflow, overwhelm queues, and reduce operational efficiency despite not being harmful. We investigate detection of these non-actionable emails in the customer-service domain, where not all incoming messages require agent intervention. Unlike traditional email spam classifiers focused on malicious or promotional content, our objective is to identify non-actionable messages that do not require attention. To address this challenge, we compare two methodological approaches: (1) a hybrid ML model, a traditional machine learning (ML) classifier enhanced with a confidence-aware, rule-augmented hybrid ML pipeline that integrates calibration-derived uncertainty zones with domain-informed override rules, and (2) a few-shot LLM-based model using prompt engineering tailored to domain-specific semantics. On our in-house dataset, experimental results demonstrate that the hybrid ML model, notably SVM, achieved a Matthews Correlation Coefficient (MCC) of 84% and 98% precision, while the GPT-4o-mini achieved 83% MCC and 98% precision better than the other free and paid LLM models. These findings highlight the effectiveness of both approaches in improving semantic-aware email classification, especially the hybrid approach detects non-actionable emails as accurately as the LLM approach while favoring lower cost, greater controllability, and seamless integration into existing email-processing systems. This work offers a practical and interpretable framework for operational use and lays the groundwork for future systems capable of adapting to evolving communication patterns in customer service settings.

### abstract

Constructing a provably feasible warehouse fulfilment schedule has historically required weeks of expert mathematical modelling — a bottleneck that places rigorous operations research beyond the reach of most mid-market logistics operations. This paper introduces Autonomous Mobile Robot — Large Language Model (AMR-LLM), to the best of our knowledge the first end-to-end agentic artificial intelligence framework that automates the full optimisation pipeline from a plain-text problem description to a deployable robot–picker co-ordination schedule, reducing expert modelling time by 99.6–99.7% without sacrificing solution feasibility. The framework integrates a large language model as a mathematical reasoning engine with five solver algorithms, simultaneously optimising order allocation, multi-agent routing, human–robot synchronisation, congestion avoidance, and battery management within a single unified formulation. Evaluated on a real warehouse dataset of 804 spatial nodes and 190,757 inventory records across instances ranging from 15 to 150 orders, the system achieves perfect code compilation, zero constraint violations, and fully feasible solutions across all experiments. A systematic hallucination analysis — the first to decompose language model errors across variable generation, constraint generation, objective construction, and code execution in an operations research context — reveals overall error rates at or below 5% on small instances, mitigated by a four-layer safeguard that eliminates variable hallucination entirely.

### abstract

We report on a systematic measurement study of unauthenticated Ollama AI inference servers exposed on the public internet. Using passive enumeration via the Shodan Internet Intelligence Platform, we identify 20,971 publicly accessible Ollama endpoints and more than 54,000 total AI inference services across more than ten backend types, all reachable with no authentication. We conduct targeted proof-of-concept probing against three representative endpoints spanning enterprise cloud, residential broadband, and commercial VPS infrastructure across three countries (USA, Sweden, Austria). In live testing, the fourth vector extracted 11 of 11 PII categories including financial credentials, medical diagnoses, and authentication passwords on the first plain-language prompt against a live production endpoint.
