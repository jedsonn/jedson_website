- title: Beyond Surface Facts: Deep Graph Augmentation and Wavelet Transform for Temporal Knowledge Graph Completion
- authors: Yifan Xue, Huixian Chen, Wanqiang Cai, Yingyao Ma, Lotfi Senhadji, Huazhong Shu, Jiasong Wu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-14
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6934169
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

Temporal knowledge graph completion aims to infer missing facts by leveraging both structural and temporal information. However, existing temporal knowledge graphs suffer from two fundamental limitations. First, they provide only surface quadruples with shallow semantic coverage, leaving out deeper semantic connections that can be derived from rich textual descriptions of entities. Second, the knowledge graphs exhibit heterogeneous temporal properties, consisting of time-invariant, short-term temporal, and long-term temporal facts, which hinder accurate temporal reasoning. To address them, we propose DeepWave, a novel framework for temporal knowledge graph completion. DeepWave consists of two key modules: (1) Deep Graph Augmentation, which leverages large language models to enrich temporal knowledge graphs with deep triples derived from entity descriptions, thereby expanding semantic coverage; and (2) Wavelet-based Temporal Relation, which decomposes relation representations via wavelet transform to jointly capture short-term and long-term temporal trends, while seamlessly integrating time-invariant facts. Extensive experiments on multiple temporal knowledge graph benchmarks demonstrate that DeepWave significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines, validating the effectiveness of semantic augmentation and wavelet-based temporal modeling.

## uid: `arxiv:2606.15999v1`

- title: U.S. Policies Unintentionally Accelerated China's Open AI Ecosystems
- authors: Wang Jin, Nadav Kunievsky, Bowen Lou, Tianshu Sun, James Evans
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-14
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.15999v1
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

Over the past decade, U.S. policies have increasingly aimed to preserve artificial intelligence (AI) leadership by promoting domestic free-market policies while controlling global technological chokepoints, particularly advanced semiconductors and computational infrastructure. These measures raised the cost of Chinese AI development, but they also increased the strategic value of open and locally adaptable AI systems. Before raising export controls on high-performance chips, both the U.S. and China promoted policies that included support for open-source AI. During the period following major U.S. export-control shocks, China increasingly embedded open-source AI into national technology strategy through proposed ecosystem building, standards coordination, and resilience-oriented deployment. Moreover, Chinese developers increased engagement with open-source large language model repositories substantially more than U.S. developers did, consistent with a shift toward open infrastructure under geopolitical constraints. Subsequently, Chinese-origin open models diffused widely through open-source communities and scientific research. Even though such models remained largely absent from U.S. patent disclosures, American commercial entities use them in open-access research, suggesting their undermeasured importance within the foundation of U.S. commercial activity. These findings suggest that technological containment policies may unintentionally accelerate open innovation ecosystems as a competitive response, with implications for global leadership in both academic and commercial artificial intelligence.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6947474`

- title: Trajectory Control for Video Diffusion Transformer with Decoupled Guidance
- authors: Cheng Lei, Jiayu Zhang, Yue Ma, Xinyu Wang, Long Chen, Liang Tang, Yiqiang Yan, Fei Su
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6947474
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Diffusion Transformer(DiT)-based video generation models exhibit strong generative capabilities. Trajectory control represents a user-friendly task in the field of controllable video generation. However, existing methods either require substantial training resources or are specifically designed for U-Net, do not take advantage of the superior performance of DiT. To address these issues, we propose a simple but effective training-free framework for trajectory control in text-to-video generation with decoupled guidance, tailored for DiT. Specifically, first, we propose foreground-background separation guidance: we use the Large Language Model (LLM) to convert user-provided prompts into foreground and background prompts, which respectively guide the generation of foreground and background regions in the video. Then, we analyze 3D full attention and explore the tight correlation between inter-token attention scores and position embedding. Based on this, we propose inter-frame Spatial-Temporal Decoupled 3D-RoPE (STD-RoPE). By modifying only foreground tokens' position embedding, STD-RoPE eliminates their cross-frame spatial discrepancies, strengthening cross-frame attention among them and thus enhancing trajectory control. Additionally, we achieve 2.5D-aware trajectory control by regulating the density of position embedding. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method outperforms previous methods in both video quality and trajectory controllability.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6946402`

- title: HOW ATTITUDE SHAPES GENERATIVE AI ADOPTION INTENTIONS AMONG GRADUATE STUDENTS
- authors: Nicolas  A. Nunez, Cindy Salvador-Marquez, Jonathan Poma-Chavez
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6946402
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

Despite attitude's theoretical centrality in technology acceptance, UTAUT-based models have largely omitted it as a mediating mechanism. This study reinstates attitude within this framework by examining whether it mediates the relationship between performance expectancy and behavioral intention in voluntary Generative AI adoption among graduate business students. Drawing on TAM and UTAUT, we analyzed survey data from 252 MBA students at an internationally accredited business school in Peru. Using ordinary least squares regression with HC3 robust standard errors and nonparametric bootstrap mediation analysis, we found that performance expectancy significantly influenced both attitude and behavioral intention. Attitude accounted for 60.6% of the total effect in the simple model and 46.3% after adjusting for effort expectancy, risk perception, prior training, use frequency, age, and sex. These results position attitude as a crucial evaluative mechanism that transforms cognitive beliefs into intended use, not a redundant construct. This study contributes to educational technology adoption research by restoring attitude's explanatory role in UTAUT-based models and offers practical implications for business schools integrating Gen AI into MBA learning environments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6946541`

- title: All That Glitters Is Not Gold? Evidence from Annual Report Aesthetics and Stock Price Crash Risk
- authors: Chuanqi Ling, Dayong Dong
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6946541
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

This study investigates whether the visual aesthetics of annual reports affect future stock price crash risk. Using a sample of Chinese A-share listed firms from 2007 to 2024, we apply a multimodal large language model to annual reports to construct a novel measure of report aesthetics. We find that firms with highly aesthetic annual reports experience significantly lower future crash risk. This negative relationship remains robust to a range of robustness and endogeneity tests. Mechanism tests suggest that report aesthetics are associated with better corporate governance, reduced managerial myopia, increased market attention, more efficient information processing, enhanced transparency, and less bad news hoarding. Finally, cross-sectional analyses reveal that the mitigating effect of report aesthetics on crash risk is more pronounced for firms facing higher technological complexity and lower default risk.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6935095`

- title: Virtual jury, real anxiety: Effects of Large Language Model on multimodal anxiety responses to a Trier Social Stress Test in Virtual Reality
- authors: Khawla AJANA, Bram Van Bockstaele, Patricia Knezevic, Amandine Verstegen, Klaas Bombeke, Bart Soenens, Peter Prinzie
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6935095
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

The Trier Social Stress Test (TSST) is a widely used and validated procedure for inducing and investigating stress responses in humans. Although virtual reality (VR) adaptations of the TSST have been developed, most have primarily replicated the in-person protocol without exploring potential additional benefits of VR. The present preregistered study investigated whether VR TSST induces anxiety in emerging adults and whether real-time socially evaluative questions generated by a Large Language Model (LLM) further modulate these responses. Sixty-two emerging adults completed either a standard VR TSST or an LLM-enhanced VR TSST. Anxiety was assessed multimodally through subjective anxiety ratings, physiological stress markers (electrodermal activity and blood volume pulse), eye gaze behavior, as well as basic psychological needs, frustration, and emotion regulation. Both VR TSST conditions demonstrated phase-dependent increases in subjective anxiety and physiological stress responses, consistent with the typical temporal pattern of social-evaluative threat. However, the addition of LLM-generated questions did not produce incremental effects on anxiety or basic psychological needs frustration. Exploratory analyses indicated that higher baseline trait anxiety and greater use of disengagement-oriented emotion regulation strategies predicted greater subjective but not physiological anxiety reactivity. Overall, our findings provide further support for the use of VR TSST paradigms to induce subjective anxiety and partial physiological stress responses in a controlled environment. While LLM-generated evaluative questions did not amplify anxiety responses in the present implementation, this study provides an initial feasibility test of integrating LLM-enhanced evaluator interactions into VR TSST and contributes to defining boundary conditions for their potential impact on anxiety responses.

- title: A Semantic-Aware Dual-Stream Framework for Spatiotemporal Trajectory-User Linking
- authors: Haolun Ding, Rong Zhang, Hang Zhang, Tuohang Li, Hongping Shu, Guisong Liu, Qiang Gao
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6946593
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Recent Trajectory-User Linking (TUL) in location-based services is a foundational task for understanding individual movement patterns, aiming to accurately associate trajectories with their corresponding users. Current solutions rely heavily on POI identifiers and sequential dynamics, thereby providing a narrow spatiotemporal insight. This paradigm yields task-specific representations that lack commonality in human mobility patterns and cross-city transferability. In this work, we propose a Semantic-Aware Trajectory-User Linking framework with city-wise transfer learning, named SATUL. In detail, SATUL introduces a pre-trained large language model (LLM) to semantically encode POI category texts and develops multi-frequency sinusoidal encoding to jointly capture temporal periodicity and spatial continuity. Furthermore, SATUL establishes cross-city transferability through a dual-stream mechanism that pre-trains a shared trajectory encoder across cities through joint domain optimization. Experimental results on real-world check-in datasets demonstrate that SATUL outperforms state-of-the-art baselines and supports cross-city transfer learning.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6946156`

- title: C-UKRE: Trust-Gated Counterfactual Evidential Reasoning for Reply-Free Rumor Detection
- authors: Jiangnan Li, Xiaofeng Cao, Yaqin Wu, Ruoyu Wang, Jiawei Zhao
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-15
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6946156
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Reply-free rumor detection requires a model to reason from source-post semantics and incomplete propagation signals while distinguishing insufficient evidence from conflicting evidence. Existing graph-based and knowledge-enhanced methods remain largely deterministic, whereas recent LLM-based approaches can generate useful auxiliary evidence but still lack explicit mechanisms for trust control and epistemic uncertainty modeling. To address this problem, we propose C-UKRE, a trust-gated counterfactual evidential reasoning framework for reply-free rumor detection. C-UKRE constructs a dual-view architecture: a reality view that encodes source-post semantics and observed propagation topology, and a counterfactual view that uses a frozen Large Language Model (LLM) to instantiate virtual expert responses as heuristic evidence. We further introduce a learnable trust gate and a Dempster-Shafer-based evidential fusion module to adaptively combine the two views and produce belief masses together with explicit epistemic uncertainty. Experiments on Twitter15, Twitter16, and PHEME under a strict reply-free setting show that C-UKRE improves the F1-score by 2.13% and 2.05% on Twitter15 and Twitter16 over the strongest baseline, while achieving the highest precision of 87.06% on PHEME. Additional analyses show that the estimated uncertainty is useful for uncertainty stratification and human-in-the-loop triage, highlighting the practical value of the proposed evidential learning framework under limited-context rumor verification.
