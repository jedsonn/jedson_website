- title: Competition Implications of Artificial Intelligence in Public Procurement
- authors: Albert Sanchez-Graells
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-26
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6884238
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

Artificial intelligence (AI) has two primary interactions with public procurement. First, procurement is the gateway for the still largely unregulated process of public sector AI adoption. Second, AI is being deployed to ‘streamline’ procurement itself. This contribution explores the competition implications of these interactions between AI and procurement, as well as the knock-on effects that AI-supported procurement can have on upstream markets. In particular, it considers competition implications of new procurement approaches concerning: the setting of technical and governance requirements; reliance on third party capabilities; unregulated AI audit and assurance mechanisms; unregulated practices such as the free embedding of AI solutions into contracted cloud or software solutions (or ‘AI creep’); as well as the risks of new forms of data bias, organisational conflicts of interest and centralisation forces arising from the deployment of AI solutions in procurement management (including ‘AI agents’). The contribution highlights emerging issues and trends, and identifies areas for future research.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6926219`

- title: Agentic AI Cyber Subversion: The Semantic Layer Integrity Attack as a New Threat Class Against the Reasoning Layer
- authors: Maureen Doyle-Spare
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-26
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6926219
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Agentic AI introduces a cyber risk that does not begin with compromised credentials, poisoned training data, prompt injection, unauthorized tool use, or anomalous output. It begins when an autonomous system resolves the meaning of a regulated term across enterprise systems before execution, and that resolved meaning diverges from the definition the institution authorized. The corrupted object is not the input, the output, the model, the tool call, or the log. It is the resolved operational meaning. This paper defines Agentic Workflow Subversion as the enterprise risk surface created when reasoning-layer drift propagates across workflows, systems, and control boundaries. It defines the Semantic Layer Integrity Attack (SLIA) as the deliberate adversarial form of that risk: a cross-system integrity attack in which an actor manipulates the semantic conditions under which an agent resolves authorization, eligibility, clearance, risk, or control status, while every contributing system continues to behave correctly. It is a failure of control integrity without system compromise. The attack is cyber-relevant because it produces a clean control record. The network is not breached. The model is not necessarily altered. The prompt may be benign. The tool call may be authorized. The output may be well formed. The audit trail may be complete. Yet execution proceeds under a corrupted operational interpretation that no human or institution authorized. It situates the attack against existing agent security controls, including identity, workload authentication, prompt and input defenses, tool governance, memory and retrieval controls, output filtering, auditability, and defense-in-depth architectures. It shows that these controls are necessary but incomplete, because they govern the artifacts around agentic reasoning rather than the meaning resolved by the reasoning layer itself. It then maps the gap against current cyber and AI governance taxonomies, including NIST adversarial machine learning, the OWASP agentic AI security corpus, MITRE ATLAS, STRIDE, the Five Eyes agentic AI security guidance, and financial sector supervisory expectations, in the disciplined posture that each framework is authoritative for the scope it declares and the reasoning layer is the adjacent surface it does not directly observe. The contribution is a cyber threat class definition and a control surface argument. Agentic Workflow Subversion names the risk surface. The Semantic Layer Integrity Attack names its adversarial exploitation path. Semantic Substrate Validation and the Semantic Control Plane (SCP), operating within the broader Agentic Governance Model (AGM), name the runtime governance surface required to detect, constrain, and evidence semantic integrity before execution proceeds. It closes with detection and test conditions, control tailoring, and supervisory implications, so that the threat class is operationally actionable for a cybersecurity function rather than only conceptually defined.

### abstract

Maritime route planning requires preference-aware decision support that balances efficiency, safety, navigational stability, and user-specific needs under dynamic conditions. Conventional methods often rely on predefined objectives, limiting support for open-ended queries and interpretable trade-off reasoning. This study proposes a retrieval-augmented LLM framework for explainable maritime route recommendation. Historical AIS trajectories are converted into structured route knowledge, including OD attributes, route-level indicators, segment-level conditions, and high-risk regions. The framework combines structured OD matching with FAISS-based semantic retrieval to handle both explicit inputs and natural-language queries. Retrieved routes are reassessed using current weather information and evaluated through LLM-based multi-criteria reasoning. Experiments on 196 OD groups with expert-labeled preferences show that the full model recommends highly preferred or preferred routes in 91.84% of cases, outperforming shortest-distance and lowest-risk baselines. Results demonstrate its ability to support flexible, preference-aware, and explainable maritime route planning.

- title: Sn doping confers ZIF-8 with exceptional photocatalytic performance for efficient emerging pollutant remediation: Band structure modulation and charge transport optimization
- authors: Ziteng Liu, Longyan Cui, Pihong Geng, Qi Yang, Zhilin Yang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-26
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7005450
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

The inherent wide band gap and high carrier recombination rate of pure-phase metal-organic framework ZIF-8 severely restrict its photocatalytic applications under solar light. In this study, a series of Sn-doped ZIF-8 composite photocatalysts were successfully synthesized via a facile, one-step, room-temperature coordination method. Characterization results indicate that the introduction of Sn disrupts the original coordination equilibrium, thereby altering the apparent morphology and pore structure of ZIF-8. This compositional fine-tuning, induced by the substitution effect, successfully introduces coexisting Sn4+/Sn2+ species and abundant surface-active sites. Optical and electrochemical tests confirm that the active sites introduced into the modified framework form unsaturated active intermediate energy levels, effectively narrowing the band gap of the material from 5.11 eV to 4.29 eV, while shifting its conduction band edge potential (ECB) negatively from -0.53 V to -0.59 V vs. NHE. Furthermore, the introduction of Sn effectively optimizes the charge transport pathways and reduces the interfacial charge transfer resistance, thereby suppressing photogenerated carrier recombination and prolonging the lifetime of photogenerated charges. Kinetic evaluation demonstrates that the optimal sample, Sn1/5-ZIF-8, achieves an apparent rate constant (kobs) of 0.01881 min-1 for ofloxacin (OFL) degradation, which is 9.65 times that of pure ZIF-8 (kobs = 0.00195 min-1). Additionally, based on quenching experiments and electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) measurements, photogenerated holes (h+) and singlet oxygen (1O2) were identified as the primary reactive species for OFL degradation. Moreover, the system exhibits excellent environmental adaptability and can effectively drive the detoxification of OFL. This study provides a fresh perspective on the functional modification of porous materials based on band structure modulation.

### abstract

Artificial intelligence systems are increasingly deployed not only to generate information but to shape, structure, and accelerate human decisions. Existing approaches to AI evaluation typically focus on technical performance, safety, transparency, or regulatory compliance. However, they rarely assess whether an AI system preserves or weakens the user's ability to make reflective, accountable, and non-manipulated decisions. This working paper proposes the Decision Integrity Index, a behavioral methodology for evaluating agentic AI systems from the perspective of human decision-making quality. Drawing on behavioral economics, research on heuristics and cognitive biases, human-AI interaction studies, and emerging AI governance frameworks, the paper introduces the concept of decision integrity as a complementary dimension of AI system assessment. The proposed methodology combines a technical readiness layer, behavioral safeguard indicators, a Human Reliance Calibration Index (HRCI), and a practical human oversight quality assessment. It is designed to identify how AI systems may amplify automation bias, framing effects, overreliance, false certainty, confirmation bias, and loss of human agency. The paper argues that AI governance should move beyond formal compliance and include measurable behavioral safeguards. The proposed methodology is intended for further empirical validation and practical application in organizational AI procurement, public-sector AI evaluation, and responsible deployment of agentic AI systems.

### abstract

We present Multimodal OCR (MOCR), a document parsing paradigm that parses text and graphics into unified textual representations. Unlike conventional OCR systems, which focus on text recognition and leave graphical regions as raster crops, MOCR treats elements beyond plain text---including tables, formulas, icons, charts, and diagrams---as first-class parsing targets and represents eligible graphics as renderable structured outputs. This paradigm offers three advantages: (1) reconstructing text and graphics in structured forms for faithful document recovery; (2) reframing document parsing from element-specific recognition as unified structure recovery; and (3) converting underused graphics into reusable code-level supervision. To operationalize MOCR at scale, we build dots.mocr, a 3B-parameter model trained through staged pretraining and supervised fine-tuning with a data engine integrating PDFs, rendered webpages, native SVG assets, and vision-language data. We evaluate dots.mocr on document parsing and structured graphics reconstruction, and further explore OCR Arena as an automated Elo-style evaluation for holistic parsing quality. On document parsing, our model achieves open-source state-of-the-art performance across benchmarks including olmOCR-Bench, MDPBench, and GlotOCR Bench, and ranks second only to Gemini 3 Pro in OCR Arena. On structured graphics reconstruction, it achieves leading image-to-SVG quality across general vector graphics, charts, UI layouts, scientific figures, and chemical diagrams. These results suggest a scalable path toward transforming document collections into richer structured supervision, with textual content faithfully parsed and eligible graphics recovered as reusable renderable code. Code and models are publicly available at \url{https://github.com/rednote-hilab/dots.mocr}.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7009891`

- title: Pseudo-label Guided Trajectory Captioning
- authors: Hang Zhou, Shengwen Li, Zhouzheng Xu, Bingyi Li, Kaiyue Liu, Yaqin Ye, Hong Yao, Junfang Gong
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-27
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7009891
- keyword hits: fine-tuning, llm

### abstract

Generating the description of a trajectory is called trajectory captioning. Previous trajectory captioning methods are derived from rule-based templates or deep-learning models. The former suffers from limited flexibility and low-quality description text, and the latter involves training or fine-tuning models with large-scale annotated trajectory-text pairs that are difficult and costly to obtain in real-world scenarios. To address these issues, this paper proposes a pseudo-label guided trajectory captioning model. The model first designs a pseudo-label generation module to automatically construct multi-view textual pseudo-labels from raw trajectory data, aiming to alleviates the problem of insufficient labeled samples. In addition, it introduces a cross-modal trajectory representation module that learns the semantic mapping between the trajectory modality and the text modality, facilitating modality alignment within a unified representation space. Finally, the trajectory description generation module integrates LLM with supervised fine-tuning and multi-source semantic fusion to generate better trajectory descriptions. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms baseline methods in both description quality and semantic expressiveness. This study provides a new technical paradigm for trajectory captioning under weakly supervised setting, and offers insights for semantic modeling and cross-modal understanding of spatiotemporal data.
