## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6788019`

- title: Optimal Advertising with Adjustable Click-through Rates and Latent Conversion Rates
- authors: Saeed Alaei, Ali Makhdoumi, Azarakhsh Malekian
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6788019
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Motivated by advertising on large language model (LLM) platforms, we study a setting in which an LLM platform interacts with K advertisers over T rounds, and in each round selects a product and designs an ad by tuning its click-through rate. Unlike traditional advertising, where ads are designed by advertisers, and conversion per click is known (making pay-perclick pricing natural), LLM platforms directly control ad design, rendering conversion per click endogenous and unknown. In particular, the conversion rate for each of these advertisers (once the user clicks) is unknown to both the advertiser and the platform, but the advertiser observes whether a conversion occurs and can strategically misreport this information to the platform. We formulate the problem of finding the social welfare maximizing policy as a (strategic) multiarmed bandit with the objective of minimizing the regret. When advertisers are not strategic in reporting their conversions, we propose a Click-UCB algorithm, establish its regret bound, and show a matching lower bound. Our algorithm uses the geometry of the mapping from clickthrough rate to conversion, along with a variant of self-normalized concentration inequalities for the maximum likelihood estimator. We then extend our results to a setting in which the advertisers are strategic and propose a policy for the platform under which, when each advertiser plays its dominant equilibrium strategy, the overall social welfare has a near-optimal regret.

- authors: Songtao Hu, Qianyue Zhang, Yiming Wang, Jun Yang, Xiaokang Wang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6795789

### abstract

Operational maritime surveillance is a decision problem, not a classication problem: agencies must choose which AIS tracks to patrol next under nite assets. We reframe AIS-based recognition as the upstream module of an end-to-end decision framework. Cross-domain experiments between DC2020 (China coast) and a GlobalFishingWatch (GFW) subset expose strongly asymmetric transfer: small-to-large ne-tuning retains 86% of from-scratch performance, whereas the reverse overts severely. An in-domain reversal between deep encoders and classical ensembles further suggests an operational crossover near 5,000 labeled segments, reported as a corpus-specic heuristic rather than a scaling law. The framework couples (i) the cross-domain recognizer, (ii) a transparent linear risk-scoring layerfusing softmax uncertainty, trac density, and an exclusive-economic-zone ag, and (iii) a GraphRAG diagnostic side-channel whose audit (58.3% KG-label leakage; hallucination on clean samples) validates excluding LLM outputs from the operational loop. Three guidelines follow: favor classical ensembles below 5,000 segments, transfer only small-to-large, and treat LLM rationales as analyst aids rather than classiers.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6789778`

- title: Token Leverage: A Framework for AI Inside the Firm
- authors: Cayman Seagraves, Stace Sirmans
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6789778
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

Studies of generative AI at work find large gains in some settings, no effect in others, and losses in others. The pattern is less puzzling if the treatment is not access or adoption, but priced inference chosen for a worker-task pair. We call this margin token leverage, billed AI inference spend per dollar of task-allocated labor cost. Inference creates value only when the task lies within the model's frontier, the worker can orchestrate and audit output, the workflow supplies useful context, governance catches errors, and workers know when to abstain. Two measurement results follow. Under voluntary firm choice, positive billed spend, effective-cost dominance, and correct firm expectations, billed token leverage is a revealed willingness-to-pay lower bound on expected AI-created operating value per task labor dollar. Access and adoption estimates instead mix chosen use, optimal abstention, forced overuse, and constrained underuse, so their sign can be positive, zero, or negative even when chosen use is valuable. AI productivity research should therefore measure worker-task inference spend rather than stopping at whether workers had or used AI.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6800603`

- title: LLM-enhanced decision support for integrated lot-streaming and AGV scheduling in the flexible flow shop
- authors: Zhangwen Huo, Qianwang Deng, Si Yang, Jingxing Zhang, Bin Luo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6800603
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

Consumer electronics manufacturing increasingly requires decision-support methods that can jointly coordinate lot streaming, production sequencing, and automated guided vehicle (AGV) transportation when jobs enter the shop at different processing stages. This paper formulates a flexible flow shop scheduling problem with non-uniform starting operations, variable sub-lots, and AGV transportation as an industrial decision-support problem for static production planning. We then develop an evolutionary method enhanced by a large language model (LLM), which serves as an archive-conditioned search operator guided by problem decomposition as well as elite and tabu solutions. Comparative experiments against heuristic, meta-heuristic, reinforcement learning-based, and LLM-driven baselines show that the proposed method can provide higher-quality scheduling decisions on 27 instances. Sensitivity analyses under processing time disturbance, transportation time inflation, and AGV availability reduction further show that the method degrades more gradually than the selected baselines. These findings indicate that the proposed method can serve as a promising decision-support mechanism for coordinating production and material handling in the studied static setting.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6787718`

- title: Do Budget Narratives Matter? Evidence From A Focal-Budget Experiment with Human and AI Respondents
- authors: Justin M. Ross, Whitney Afonso, Denvil Duncan
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6787718
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

Advice on narrative framing in budget presentations is abundant, but evidence for its impact is limited. This paper uses a series of experiments to investigate whether a department budget narrative successfully acquires higher budget allocations when it adopts common narrative strategies. Specifically, we focus on two strategies: linking proposed department budgets to the larger strategic goals of the organization (prioritybased budgeting) and identifying performance indicators ex ante that propose a form of accountability and transparency (performance-based budgeting). We propose a novel experimental design for budgeting experiments that implements "focal" budgets in the presence of multiple alternative outcomes that retains internally valid causal identification while also reducing external validity concerns related to instrument-specific bias. The design is applied to three groups of experimental subjects: human local government budget practitioners, human subjects recruited via an online subject pool, and a set of AI respondents that vary according to personality traits built from the 2024 General Social Survey. Both human and AI respondents systematically agree that neither priority-based budgeting nor performance-based budgeting strategies prove persuasive to budget allocators-respondents are unresponsive to the narrative framing when deciding how much to allocate to a given department. Second, respondents recognizably perceive the treatments as accomplishing their intended framing goals, confirming that the experimental instruments work as designed. Our results suggest that budget narrative devices have limited influence in the end stages of the budget process, pointing toward the supply side of budget presentations-how performance indicators and priority linkages shape the proposals themselves-as a promising avenue for future research. We provide a Model Context Protocol that allows users to replicate our results and further interrogate our data with natural language through a large language model.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6800339`

- title: Does Artificial Intelligence Facilitate Environmental Performance? Firm-Level Evidence from LLM-Based Measurement
- authors: Liang Wang, Ling-Yun He
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-20
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6800339
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Rapid advances in artificial intelligence (AI) are reshaping corporate operations, yet its implications for firms' environmental performance remain underexplored. This study investigates whether and how AI adoption contributes to environmental sustainability by constructing a novel firm-level AI index using natural language processing on annual reports of Chinese A-share listed companies from 2009 to 2022. Empirical analyses reveal that AI adoption significantly improves corporate environmental performance, and this result is robust to alternative model specifications, subsample exclusions, and instrumental variable estimations. Empirical evidence indicates that improving productivity, strengthening governance quality, and fostering green innovation serve as the key channels through which AI adoption promotes firms' environmental sustainability. Meanwhile, the effects are not uniform across AI subfields, with robotics and planning-oriented systems generating substantial improvements in environmental performance, while language- and vision-based applications exhibit adverse impacts. Further heterogeneity analyses indicate that the environmental benefits of AI are more pronounced in state-owned enterprises, in provinces with lower levels of marketization, and in regions with weaker regulatory enforcement. Overall, these findings highlight AI's potential as a catalyst for corporate sustainability and provide new evidence on the role of emerging technologies in advancing green development.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6733402`

- title: The Dual Nature of LLM Persona: Aggregated Tendencies and Frame-Dependent Geometry
- authors: Yuan Yuan
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-21
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6733402
- keyword hits: gpt-4, llm

### abstract

Evaluations of LLM personas via psychometric questionnaires typically rely on aggregate scores, discarding within-instance correlation structure. We test whether this geometric structure is intrinsic or frame-dependent. Constructing withininstance correlation matrices from IPIP-50 responses, we analyze geometry on SPD manifolds under manipulated question orderings in GPT-4o simulating American and Chinese-American personas. We find that persona expression comprises two dissociable components: aggregated features (Big Five scores) degrade under randomization (21% drop) but are frame-robust; geometric features (SPD manifold) collapse under frame misalignment (42% drop) but recover substantially (to 84%) under shared frames, surpassing aggregated features (76%). This collapse-recovery pattern reveals that persona geometry is not intrinsic but a frame-dependent coordination pattern encoding information invisible to aggregation. Our findings establish a dual-nature framework for LLM personas-frame-dependent geometry versus frame-robust aggregates-necessitating frame-aware evaluation and challenging static trait conceptions.
