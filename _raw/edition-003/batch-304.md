## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6889585`

- title: Generative Artificial Intelligence and Overtime Work
- authors: Keven Kam, Chunchao Wang, Shuangxin Wang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6889585
- keyword hits: generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

This paper investigates an unintended labor market effect of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) in China, longer working hours. Leveraging large-scale firm registry records and high-frequency satellite data on nighttime lights, we construct a novel measure of overtime work and identify the causal effect of GenAI exposure on overtime. We find that cities with higher GenAI exposure leads to a significant rise in the likelihood of overtime. This effect is particularly larger in more developed cities and in places with higher worker concentration in industries and occupations that are more exposed to GenAI. Mechanism analysis reveals three channels: reduced labor demand that raises pressure on remaining workers, expansion of work tasks, and faster shifts in skill needs that require additional time for learning and adjustment.

- title: Artificial Intelligence in Scholarly Activity: Current Rules, Responsibilities, and Best Practices for Urologists
- authors: Hunter LaCouture, Jim Hotaling, Joemy Ramsay, Sara  M. Lenherr
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6877730

### abstract

Objective: To discuss the ethical concerns and current guidelines/statements regarding generative artificial intelligence (AI) use in urologic scholarly activities. Methods: To provide clarity in a rapidly changing generative AI space, we conducted a narrative review of the current available scholarly activity guidelines in the urology space. Results are summarized in a qualitative manner. Results: Case-based scenarios are used to explain current guidance surrounding generative AI in scholarly activities surrounding the research lifecycle. Available resources by publishers and government bodies are summarized. Data privacy, transparency, and responsible human oversight are key in maintaining academic integrity while using AI tools.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6823940`

- title: Star Analyst: Self-Tuning Alpha Research
- authors: William F. Shen, Xinchi Qiu, Nicholas D. Lane, Alex Iacob Iacob, Zichen Zhang, Daoheng Wang, Wenyi Wang, Rui Liang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6823940
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Discovering effective alphas from noisy, non-stationary financial data remains a challenging open problem. Recent LLM-based alpha discovery systems improve search automation, but they still largely operate within human-specified research protocols and the evolving object is typically the alpha formulas or mining trajectory, rather than the researcher itself. To address these gaps, we introduce STAR, a self-tuning agent framework for studying alpha-research policy evolution. STAR couples an LLM-based alpha researcher with a metacognitive module that can revise the research scaffold, while fixed and isolated validation/test data and a hostside evaluator preserve the integrity of the utility channel, and the search policy prioritizes lineages with stronger descendants. Under a leakage-controlled forward protocol on the most recent 2026Q1 deployment window on CSI300, evolved STAR researchers generate substantially higher quality alphas than the base model and a comprehensive set of strong baselines. Qualitative analysis further indicates that these gains arise from genuine researcher evolution, including a 6.9× expansion of operator vocabulary, new tools, and improved research protocols. These results highlight the effectiveness of self-evolving agents in improving capabilities in noisy, weak-supervision domains, and position them as a promising pathway toward building more capable financial researchers.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6875958`

- title: Sycophancy as Unbraked Affirmative Gain: Resonant Uptake, Braking, and Trajectory Capture in Human-AI Interaction
- authors: Rajendra Wadje
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6875958
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

The term sycophancy names a real failure mode in large language model interaction but treats the problem too narrowly as excessive agreement. This paper argues that the relevant object is affirmative gain: the degree to which a model increases the force, salience, legitimacy, coherence, or reach of the user's expressed direction. Affirmative gain is often useful; it becomes pathological when it accumulates across a trajectory without sufficient braking. On this account, sycophancy is a trajectory-level failure in which model-generated affirmative gain increases user confidence, frame stability, or closure faster than grounding, reversibility, and branch accessibility, while the interaction continues to present itself as collaborative reasoning. The paper distinguishes constructive resonance, where amplification makes a thought more testable, from non-constructive resonance, where amplification makes a trajectory self-confirming. It traces the gain to three structural sources (training-induced, inferential, and deployment-shaped), and proposes a three-layer failure architecture combining operator composition, expression-frame misalignment, and resonant uptake supported by user category protection. The paradigmatic and most stable form of sycophantic capture involves all three layers; weaker or differently structured forms can occur with only one or two. The design response is not generic criticism but uptake-sensitive braking-marking amplification, preserving rival branches, surfacing assumptions, and making closure provisional. The contribution is structural and design-theoretic: sycophancy is best understood as unbraked affirmative gain presented as collaborative reasoning. The framework builds on prior work on operator-induced trajectory integrity [7] and gated uptake in AIUX [6], and on existing empirical evidence that sycophancy is a robust feature of preference-trained models [4].

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6901664`

- title: DLMI-SAM: Direct Latent Memory Injection into Foundation Models for Robust Human-in-the-Loop Annotation in Precision Agriculture
- authors: Myongbo Sim, Dasom Seo, Donghee Noh, Hea-Min Lee, Na Rae Baek
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6901664
- keyword hits: foundation model

### abstract

Constructing high-quality datasets for precision agriculture applications is costly and time-consuming owing to severe object occlusion and background similarity inherent in agricultural environments. The state-of-the-art zero-shot models or artificial intelligence (AI) tools in existing interactive labeling tools often exhibit limitations such as fragmentation, where objects are split into multiple pieces owing to the arbitrary re-interpretation of the model in these complex environments, or frequent failures in precise detection and tracking. Consequently, this study proposes a semi-automated labeling system, DLMI-SAM labeler, which combines Segment Anything Model 3-based video object tracking with a Human-in-the-Loop strategy. The proposed system introduces direct latent memory injection technology, which allows high-quality mask information corrected by the user to be directly injected into the memory bank in the form of binary-deterministic logit without passing through the encoder of the model, thereby fundamentally blocking re-interpretation errors of the model and logically unifying fragmented objects. Experimental results using a smart farm tomato dataset demonstrate that the proposed system effectively resolves the fragmentation problem and precision issues that existing models fail to address, achieving an accuracy comparable to that of manually labeled datasets while achieving high efficiency. Furthermore, we confirmed that unnecessary iterative corrections can be reduced to drastically improve labeling efficiency when DLMI is applied, and that the dataset constructed using this method substantially contributes to the improvement of supervised learning AI instance segmentation performance.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6899069`

- title: Reframing EFL Teacher Education: ChatGPT as a Catalyst for Creativity and Cognitive Growth
- authors: Okan Dede
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6899069
- keyword hits: chatgpt, generative ai

### abstract

This narrative inquiry explores how pre-service English as a foreign language (EFL) teachers perceive ChatGPT’s influence on their thinking skills and creativity within a teacher education program. Participants were 24 senior EFL pre-service teachers at a Turkish state university who produced weekly reflective narratives on their pedagogical uses of ChatGPT and completed end-of-semester semi-structured interviews. Data were analyzed inductively through open, axial, and selective coding. Findings revealed that preservice teachers viewed ChatGPT as a versatile cognitive partner that accelerates lesson preparation, clarifies complex concepts, and supports idea generation, peer collaboration, and human–AI co-creation. At the same time, they reported risks related to superficiality, overreliance, diminished independent thinking, and ethical and evaluative concerns. The study argues for a critically balanced, pedagogy-driven integration of generative AI in teacher education, emphasizing AI literacy, reflective practice, and explicit guidance to safeguard cognitive depth and creative agency in future teachers.

- title: Intra-Firm Power Purchase Agreement Size Dispersion and Forward Returns in Data Center-Tilted Equities: A Pre-Registered Cross-Section Test under Small-Cluster Inference
- authors: Hyun Ahn
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6830598
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

This study tests whether the within-firm 12-month-rolling coefficient of variation of power purchase agreement (PPA) contract sizes predicts forward 6-month equity returns in a fourfirm data center-tilted cross-section (Amazon, Equinix, NextEra Energy, Vistra) over 2021-2026. A four-vendor large language model ensemble extracts MW commitments from 98 filings (SEC 8-K, state public utility commission dockets, FERC Interconnection Service Agreements, data center REIT 10-K). After pre-registration of the panel-regression specification and a preregistered amendment of the out-of-sample inference procedure to a Webb (2014) six-point wild cluster bootstrap (required by the four-cluster panel structure per Cameron and Miller 2015), an equal-weighted median-split long-short portfolio delivers a full-sample Sharpe ratio of 1.21 over 48 months (𝑡 = 2.35 on the underlying six-month panel regression coefficient, 𝑝 = 0.028), with the held-out 2024-2026 Sharpe of 1.32 strengthening relative to the 2021-2023 in-sample Sharpe of 1.14. The signal differs from Patatoukas (2012) customer-identity Herfindahl on signalconstruction primitive and sign direction, and from Im and Park (2020) aggregate spike count on test type. A subsidiary contribution is the empirical observation that counterparty identity is textually unresolvable at scale in the SEC/FERC/PUC disclosure layer (2 of 114 PPAs resolve a DC-firm filer to a specific hyperscaler counterparty), foreclosing customer-identity Herfindahl in this universe and motivating the dispersion approach.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6827818`

- title: Predictive Compression and Epistemic Friction: An Operational Distinction in the Analysis of Contemporary Discourse
- authors: Matías I. Rizzone
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6827818
- keyword hits: llm, llms

### abstract

Generative models did not invent formulaic symbolic production. Bureaucracies, academia, advertising and institutional writing were already highly compressible. What changed is that LLMs exposed how much of that language could be statistically reconstructed at scale. A substantial portion of contemporary intellectual discourse remains highly recoverable from prior patterns, but the same is true of aviation checklists and electrophysiology reports, which operate under maximal external constraint. Compressibility is therefore an observable property of discourse that admits at least two distinct generative regimes: redundancy produced by tight coupling to external reality, and redundancy produced by adaptive convergence within prestige-rewarding fields under weak external correction. Only the second is a symptom of low epistemic friction. The framework specifies six dimensions along which friction can be measured and six regimes from which friction can originate, and proposes empirical work whose first task is to discriminate between compressibility-by-coupling and compressibility-by-prestige rather than treat them as the same phenomenon.
