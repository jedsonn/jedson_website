## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6557018`

- title: A Modular Multi-Stream Perception and Deterministic Fusion Framework for Takeover Prompting in Autonomous Driving
- authors: Vatsal Thakkar
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6557018
- keyword hits: prompting

### abstract

Takeover management in partially automated driving requires timely assessment of driver state, vehicle behavior, and roadway context. This paper presents a modular framework that combines three perception streams: an interior-camera driver-behavior classifier, an inertialsensor classifier for coarse driving style, and a road-facing vision pipeline that combines object tracking, segmentation, and geometric proximity reasoning. The outputs of these modules are fused by a deterministic decision stage composed of a categorical lookup table, recent-history aggregation, and a takeover hold mechanism for sustained critical conditions. The proposed framework was implemented as a Python-based prototype with loosely coupled modules and explicit intermediate outputs. In contrast to end-to-end learned decision policies, the proposed design exposes the decision path at each stage and allows direct inspection of thresholds, category mappings, and temporal logic. The focus of this paper is the system architecture, mathematical formulation, and implementation structure of the prototype. The paper is therefore positioned as a methodology and system description rather than as a benchmarkoriented evaluation study.

- title: The Sovereignty Crisis: AI, Workforce Atrophy, and Systemic Risk in the Agentic Era A Policy-Technical White paper for Institutional, Infrastructure, and Governance Leaders
- authors: Emily J. Barnes PhD
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6565538
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Artificial intelligence has rapidly transitioned from experimental capability to operational infrastructure, outpacing the evolution of governance systems, workforce development, and institutional control. While AI delivers measurable gains in task-level productivity, emerging evidence points to uneven value realization, increased system dependency, and erosion of human expertise. This paper introduces the sovereignty crisis , a systemic condition in which institutions improve output faster than they preserve the capacity to understand, audit, and govern AI-mediated operations. Drawing on interdisciplinary evidence, the paper develops a framework linking workforce atrophy, cognitive overreliance, technical vulnerability, and infrastructure concentration into a unified model of systemic risk. The analysis argues that these dynamics produce conditions of accelerated fragility , where performance improvements mask declining institutional control. To address this, the paper proposes the Red Switch Mandate , a governance framework emphasizing human mastery, auditability, constrained privilege, diversified dependencies, and operational fallback. The central claim is that the defining tradeoff of the agentic era is not innovation versus regulation, but speed versus sovereignty .

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6689376`

- title: A Comparative Study of Convolutional Neural Networks and Vision Transformers for Quantitative Phase Analysis from X-Ray Diffraction
- authors: Yi Zhang, Stefanie Eulitz, Aleksander Michalak, Daniel Vollprecht, Lars Mikelsons
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6689376
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Reliable quantitative phase analysis (QPA) of mineral residues---such as bottom ash from waste incineration and metallurgical slags---is essential for resource recovery and process control, yet traditional methods such as Rietveld refinement are labor-intensive and sensitive to user expertise. Deep learning offers a route to automate XRD-based QPA, but prior architectural comparisons of convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and Vision Transformers (ViTs) have treated phase classification and fraction regression as separate stages; a joint multi-task formulation with direct, controlled architectural comparison has not been reported. We train a single multi-task model that simultaneously classifies phase presence and regresses phase fractions on mixed-phase XRD patterns, and compare a 1D-CNN against a ViT under identical conditions using a large simulation dataset of patterns spanning seven crystalline phases and a small set of real laboratory measurements of four reference materials. Four evaluation scenarios---in-domain simulated, in-domain real, zero-shot transfer, and transfer learning with fine-tuning---explicitly quantify the synthetic-to-real domain gap. Both architectures achieve near-perfect classification and regression on simulated data. On real measurements, the ViT outperforms the CNN overall, reducing RMSE by 31\% and MAE by 35\%, while the CNN retains an advantage for phases with sharp, well-resolved peaks and transfers more robustly in the zero-shot setting. Fine-tuning on a small set of real labeled measurements largely closes the domain gap for both architectures, confirming the practical viability of the simulation-to-real transfer pipeline for automated mineral QPA.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6690319`

- title: The Neural Shift Agentic AI, Cross-Asset Dynamics, and the Regulatory Black Box in Modern Finance
- authors: Clara El Rif
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6690319
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has transitioned from an experimental tool to the "central nervous system" of the global financial infrastructure. This study explores the emergence of agentic AIautonomous systems capable of independent decision-making in high-stakes environments such as credit approval and fraud detection. By utilizing Python-based analytical frameworks to examine historical data, the research highlights AI's efficacy in identifying complex correlations between traditional assets like gold and volatile digital assets like Bitcoin. However, the findings reveal a stark paradox: while AI enhances operational speed and market insights, it simultaneously facilitates sophisticated new fraud methodologies, with projected global losses expected to rise from $23 billion to $58 billion. Furthermore, despite aggressive investment, only 8% of major banks report a clear return on investment (ROI), largely due to the "black box" nature of AI decision-making. The research concludes that for AI to achieve sustainable integration, the industry must prioritize "explainable AI" (XAI) and develop robust regulatory frameworks that balance rapid innovation with transparency and consumer protection.

- title: Wrapper Architecture as Coordination Device: Multiple Equilibria in Autonomous-Agent Organizations
- authors: Ian Staley
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-02
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6697218
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

Decentralized autonomous organizations (DAOs) and AI-agent systems combine cryptographic execution with blockchain-based governance, yet observed organizations almost universally combine these mechanisms with a conventional legal entity-a foundation, statutory DAO form, or limited liability wrapper. I develop a stylized model in which token-holders jointly determine wrapper choice and governance concentration, generating multiple equilibria: an inefficient trap in which the wrapper coalition cannot form because no holder will absorb the front-loaded fixed cost alone, and an efficient wrapper equilibrium in which the coalition reaches scale and amortizes fixed costs effectively. The trap is an empirically grounded coordination problem rather than an analytical artifact, and a global-games selection argument identifies the threshold at which institutional design tips the system between equilibria. The framework reframes the CFTC v. Ooki DAO ruling, the Wyoming DAO LLC and DUNA statutes, and AI legal personhood debates as questions of equilibrium selection rather than of substantive cost allocation, and bounds the "Coasean singularity" claim that AI agents dramatically reduce transaction frictions.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6695983`

- title: Fine tuning of Co3O4-encapsulated mesoporous silica as the core@shell bifunctional catalyst toward efficient CH4 carboxylation using CO2 to CH3COOH
- authors: Hyogeun Yang, Seungdon Kwon, Woosung Leem, Gyeongmook Lim, Jeong-Chul Kim, Kyungsu Na
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-02
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6695983
- keyword hits: fine tuning

### abstract

Recently, it was reported that CO2 can serve as a carboxylating agent for the selective conversion of CH4 into CH3COOH using Co3O4 as a bifunctional catalyst. However, despite the sequential activation of both reactants, the practical CH3COOH productivity remained insufficient for industrial applications. Herein, a series of Co3O4-encapsulated mesoporous silica catalysts (denoted as Co3O4@mSiO2) were synthesized. These bifunctional core@shell architectures featured precisely tuned core diameters and shell thicknesses, achieved by systematic control of the precursor ratios. TEM and XRD analyses confirmed the successful encapsulation of nanocrystalline Co3O4 within the mesoporous silica framework, with dimensions ranging from 4.7–18.8 nm (core) and 16.9–60.1 nm (shell). Catalytic evaluations revealed that the CH3COOH yield is governed by a synergistic interplay between the core and shell dimensions. Specifically, the optimized architecture (9.9 nm core and 24.4 nm shell) delivered a peak yield of 194.2 μmol gCo3O4−1, significantly outperforming counterparts with smaller cores or thinner shells. Ex situ FT-IR spectroscopy elucidated that this optimal configuration maximized CO2 activation as surface carbonate species, facilitating the subsequent reaction with CH4. Furthermore, the mesoporous silica shell effectively suppressed sintering, ensuring structural integrity and stable performance over 10 consecutive reaction cycles.
