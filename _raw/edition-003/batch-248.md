- title: Enterprise Violation Prediction Based on Multi-Agent and Multimodal Graph Neural Network
- authors: Jinlong Wang, Yingmin liu, Shupeng Li, Xiaoyun Xiong, Hao-Ran Zhao, Zhihan Lyu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2025-10-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5572312
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

Accurately predicting corporate violations is of great importance for safeguarding investor interests and assisting securities regulatory authorities. As a result, AI-driven approaches for corporate compliance risk prediction have attracted increasing attention. However, conventional methods often suffer from limitations such as single-dimensional risk modeling and a lack of interpretability. To address these issues, this paper proposes a novel violation prediction framework based on multi-agent systems and multimodal Graph Neural Networks. Specifically, we introduce the MA-ERP (Multi-Agent Enterprise Risk Propagation) algorithm, which leverages large language model-based multi-agent collaboration to quantify heterogeneous and multimodal risk factors. These factors are then integrated into a personalized PageRank algorithm to simulate the inter-enterprise risk propagation process. Furthermore, we develop the MNF-GNN (Multimodal Node Feature Fusion Graph Neural Network), which employs a multimodal node feature encoder to extract and fuse diverse dimensions of corporate risk features. By stacking multiple layers of Relational Graph Attention Networks, the model captures global structural information across the graph, leading to enhanced predictive performance. Finally, we construct an interpretable knowledge graph to decompose the corporate risk landscape visually. The experimental results show that the method proposed in this paper demonstrates superior performance compared to traditional methods in terms of accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score. Note: ​ This manuscript is currently under consideration at Pattern Recognition Letters .
