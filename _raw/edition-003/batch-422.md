## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7138916`

- title: Diagnosis and management of lung cancer associated with cystic airspaces (LCCA): a British Thoracic Oncology Group consensus statement
- authors: Matthew Evison, Mohammed Asif, Anna Bibby, John Edwards, Alex Haragan, Georgia Hyde, Christopher  S. Johns, Ashok Kar
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7138916
- keyword hits: prompting

### abstract

Objectives. Lung cancer associated with cystic airspaces (LCCA) is a recognised but under-characterised presentation of lung cancer, reported in approximately 1–4% of cases, yet it is not included in national or international guidelines. This generates uncertainty in terminology, diagnosis, staging and management, and contributes to missed or delayed screen-detected cancers. The British Thoracic Oncology Group (BTOG) convened a multidisciplinary group to develop UK consensus recommendations.Materials and methods. A multidisciplinary working group spanning respiratory medicine, radiology, surgery, oncology and histopathology, with patient representation and nominees from relevant professional societies, was assembled through an open expression of interest. Nine domains were pre-specified. Statements were generated and agreed upon using a modified nominal group technique (silent generation, round-robin, clarification, vote) at a face-to-face meeting, followed by an online confirmatory ratification vote open to the wider working group. A 5-point Likert scale was used; consensus was defined a priori as ≥90% agreement (agree or strongly agree), rejection as ≤50%, with 50–89% prompting modification and re-vote.Results. Consensus was achieved for all nine statements, covering terminology, radiology reporting, radiological surveillance, diagnostic work-up, clinical staging, curative- and palliative-intent treatment, pathology reporting and research priorities. The group endorsed the single term “lung cancer associated with cystic airspaces (LCCA)”. Eight statements reached consensus at the first vote; the clinical-staging statement (Statement 5) was modified after the first round (86% agreement) and achieved consensus on re-vote (95%). Recommendations include reporting of whole-lesion and largest-solid-component dimensions within radiological reports, recording the conventional (solid-component) stage while also documenting whole-lesion size and recognising the limitations of current staging, planning curative therapy to the whole lesion, and caution in using clinical T-stage to drive neoadjuvant therapy.Conclusion. These first multidisciplinary consensus recommendations provide pragmatic guidance for LCCA and a framework for the prospective data collection required to inform future management.

- title: Regenerative Systems Thinking for the Agentic Enterprise Reddy's 21 Leverage Points for Governing Intelligence, Capital, and Life
- authors: Surendra Reddy
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7023239
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Artificial intelligence is becoming agentic, with autonomous and semi-autonomous systems now perceiving, deciding, coordinating, and acting inside enterprises and institutions rather than only storing or predicting. This paper argues that the consequences of that shift depend less on the capability of the technology than on the systems of purpose, governance, ownership, capital, and narrative into which intelligence is embedded, because AI amplifies the logic of the system that deploys it. Building on Donella Meadows's twelve leverage points, and on a prior fifteen-point regenerative extension that added capital flows, ownership, and narrative, the paper develops a twenty-one-point framework for the agentic age by adding six levers: a governance control plane and five agentic-intelligence levers covering agency, intent alignment, learning, human sovereignty, and interoperability. The levers are organized into five levels, from foundational parameters to civilizational levers, and are treated as an integrated architecture rather than independent controls. The paper applies the framework across enterprise, healthcare, education, public institutions, capital and venture creation, and food systems, distinguishes hard, soft, and smart controls, and offers a diagnostic question for each lever. The central claim is that regenerative outcomes require governing intelligence in service of life rather than embedding it in extractive logic.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7031218`

- title: Engaging Customers, Straining Infrastructure: GenAI Personalization as an Operational Decision
- authors: Zhen Liu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7031218
- keyword hits: agentic, generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) is transforming customer engagement through conversational assistants, personalized recommendations, AI-generated content, and agentic customerservice systems. These activities create marketing value, but they also require large-scale realtime inference computation. This paper develops a dynamic operations-marketing model in which AI personalization intensity is an endogenous marketing decision that generates inference demand and infrastructure stress. A platform jointly chooses AI personalization intensity and geographically distributed workload migration across two inference regions. Higher AI personalization improves customer engagement but increases computational load. Workload migration mitigates local congestion but creates cross-regional ripple effects through shared infrastructure, routing systems, and distributed orchestration. We characterize the platform's Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation, derive four structural propositions, and show that (i) optimal AI personalization decreases with infrastructure stress, (ii) optimal migration responds to congestion asymmetry across regions, (iii) ripple effects amplify system-wide operational volatility, and (iv) decentralized AI engagement generates shared infrastructure externalities relative to the social optimum. A closed-form linear-quadratic benchmark with an explicit Riccati solution clarifies the economic forces linking AI engagement, inference demand, and operational congestion. The framework highlights a new operations-marketing interface: GenAI transforms customer engagement from a primarily informational activity into a computationally intensive operational decision with shared infrastructure externalities.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7037438`

- title: When the Customer is an Algorithm: A Demand-Side Theory of the Machine Customer
- authors: Paul F. Accornero
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7037438
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

Commerce is undergoing a structural rupture that the dominant frameworks misread. As AI agents move from advising consumers to executing their purchases, the customer the firm must win is no longer a human but a machine acting on a human's behalf. This paper formalizes that rupture as the Shopper Schism: the structural separation, in delegated categories, of the consumer, who experiences value, from the shopper, who executes the purchase. We argue that the prevailing supply-side reading of AI in commerce, in which sellers adapt their channels and content, obscures the deeper event, which is demand-side. When an agent buys, demand itself is generated, filtered, and expressed by a non-human actor with different selection logic, latency, switching cost, and loyalty mechanics than a person. We anchor the resulting theory to a single primary construct, Agent Intent Optimization (AIO®), the practice of competing for the buying agent's selection rather than the human's attention; AIO-Commerce is introduced as the derived practitioner label for the category that AIO governs. We distinguish this discipline from search and conversion optimization, position it against the emerging industry vocabulary of business-to-AI selling and agent optimization, and derive a set of testable propositions for commercial strategy, brand equity, and governance in markets where the customer is algorithmic. The contribution is an integrative demand-side theory of the machine customer that formalizes a buyer whose existence the field already recognizes but has not theorized, and a conceptual anchor for a research stream on selling to the machine.

## uid: `arxiv:2607.20019v1`

- title: EvoDRC: A Self-Evolving Agentic Framework for Automated DRC Violation Repair
- authors: Bing-Yue Wu, Chia-Tung Ho, Haoyu Yang, Brucek Khailany, Vidya A. Chhabria
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20019v1
- keyword hits: agentic, llm

### abstract

Design rule check (DRC) closure remains a major bottleneck in advanced-node physical design. Although detailed routers are rule-aware, residual design rule violations (DRVs) often require manual engineering change order iterations. Automating this process is challenging because repairs must account for complex geometric interactions, preserve circuit connectivity, and avoid introducing new violations. We present EvoDRC, a skill-evolution framework for agentic block-level DRC repair. EvoDRC initializes layer-specific repair skills using knowledge distilled from an unrelated reference design and continuously evolves these skills using traceable repair experience collected from the target design. EvoDRC decomposes the layout into bounded repair regions and assigns an LLM repair agent to each region. Local DRC analysis, connectivity-checking, and impact-preview tools provide feedback on proposed modifications. Repair operations and their resulting DRV changes are stored in a knowledge database and used to evolve the repair skills. Experiments on seven block-level designs from the DAC26 DRC Benchmark show that EvoDRC achieves a 73.5\% overall reduction compared to the reported baseline.

- title: Overview of FinMMEval 2026 Task 1: Multilingual Financial Multiple-Choice Question Answering
- authors: Zhuohan Xie, Yuyang Dai, Rania Elbadry, Vanshikaa Jani, Georgi Georgiev, Dimitar Dimitrov, Fan Zhang, Xueqing Peng
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-22
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19856v1
- keyword hits: llm, prompting

### abstract

FinMMEval 2026 Task 1 evaluates multilingual financial multiple-choice question answering in English, Chinese, Arabic, and Hindi. The task tests whether systems can select the correct answer to finance questions involving domain terminology, numerical interpretation, and conceptual financial reasoning across languages and scripts. The final-test set contains 800 questions, with 200 questions per language; gold answers were withheld during submission, and each language was ranked independently by accuracy. The final leaderboards contain 13 English, 11 Chinese, 11 Arabic, and 10 Hindi ranked submissions. Top accuracies range from 92.0% in Hindi to 97.5% in English and Arabic, with the same leading teams appearing near the top across all four languages. The documented systems used retrieval augmentation, direct answer-option scoring, language-specific prompting, selective self-consistency, confidence checks, and LLM-based review stages.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7073378`

- title: When AI Becomes the Consumer: Solving the Depopulation Crisis
- authors: Taichi Yamaguchi
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-23
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7073378
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

This paper introduces a novel macroeconomic paradigm that endogenizes Artificial Intelligence (AI) agents fundamentally as autonomous end-user consumers rather than mere supply-side inputs. Facing acute population decline, traditional growth models dictate economic contraction due to shrinking domestic demand. We challenge this premise by constructing a general equilibrium framework featuring a large, heterogeneous population of AI agents endowed with modified Stone-Geary utility functions, supported by a realistic human demographic trajectory that stabilizes at a sustainable lower bound. The central contribution of this paper is the formalization of AI as a primary driver of effective demand, establishing a baseline survival cost (electricity) and idiosyncratic discretionary preferences for "Human-Originated Authenticity". While AI cannot biologically consume physical goods, their market transactions generate valid effective demand and direct income redistribution to human agents. Under an optimal Ramsey taxation scheme, we prove that per-capita human wealth expands monotonically as the human population declines toward its long-run steady state, transforming human economic activity from a survival necessity into a mechanism for genuine, culturally enriching self-actualization without relying on infinite divergence paradoxes.

### abstract

Digital forensic examiners and incident-response personnel are routinely required to come into contact with disturbing stimuli, including extortive communications, breach forensic evidence, malware remnants, and insider threat-related evidence. Secondary Traumatic Stress (STS) has been documented in these workers at rates comparable to first responders. STS researchers have documented a stark need for primary preventative research, stating that no systematic studies have been conducted to design STS interventions which reduce the level of traumatic stimuli exposure, as most STS research focuses on the treatment of stress symptoms. A growing body of work has focused on building partially autonomous agentic AI systems for security operations, spanning both offensive and defensive triage. Surprisingly, this work only evaluates the effectiveness of the systems on the tasks they are designed to complete and ignores the effects on the mental health of the personnel who oversee the operations and/or examine the outcomes of the operations. We identified a cluster of existing systems, which span the UK law enforcement framework, are open-source, and peer AGENTIC AI TRIAGE AND SECONDARY TRAUMATIC STRESS 2 reviewed, which suggest the AI-mediated reduction of exposure and protection of the analysts' mental health. These systems, however, do not evaluate this assumption, and the stated objective remains the motivation for using the systems. We suggest that the design of systems to allow for the graded control of exposure to traumatic stimuli may serve as an intervention, whereby agentic AI performs severity scoring, informative summarization, and redaction of traumatic stimuli before human review. We also suggest a two-phase research approach that focuses on the technical validation of systems and a controlled study to evaluate STS using the Secondary Traumatic Stress Scale. This serves as a conceptual research agenda to integrate these disciplines (the study of traumatic psychology, agentic AI system design, and forensic AI applications) for the first time.
