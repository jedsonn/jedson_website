## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6716658`

- title: Agentic AI in Agriculture: Towards Automatic Farming Systems
- authors: Anna Bonifacio, Irene Iele, Giulia Romoli, Matteo Tortora, Paolo Soda
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6716658
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Agriculture is increasingly challenged by climate variability, resource scarcity, and the need for more efficient and sustainable farm management. Although artificial intelligence has shown strong potential in tasks such as crop monitoring, disease diagnosis, irrigation support, and field robotics, most current systems remain task-specific and operate as isolated analytical or decision-support modules. This paper presents a prospective view of agentic AI in agriculture as an emerging systems paradigm for automatic farming. We distinguish AI-assisted agriculture from agentic agricultural systems, organize recent literature into four directions, and propose a framework for automatic farming systems centered on multimodal perception, specialized decision components, orchestration, bounded actuation, and continuous feedback under human oversight. We also discuss the main challenges for real-world deployment, including partial observability, heterogeneity, multi-objective decision-making, safety, and evaluation. The paper argues that the next step for agricultural AI is not only improved task-level performance, but the design of coherent farm-scale systems able to perceive, reason, and act under real operational constraints.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6696138`

- title: Beyond the Principal-Agent Paradox: A Theory of Governance Failure and Mechanism Design in Agentic AI Systems
- authors: Albert Adusei Brobbey
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6696138
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Principal-agent theory has provided the dominant framework for understanding governance failure in organizational settings for five decades. This paper argues that agentic AI systems produce a structural inversion of the classical principal-agent problem that renders conventional governance mechanisms categorically inadequate, not merely insufficient. We term this the Agentic Principal-Agent Inversion: a condition in which the human overseer retains nominal authority but loses informational parity, processing parity, and interpretive capacity simultaneously, creating a triple asymmetry that conventional principal-agent governance tools cannot resolve. Drawing on principal-agent theory, bounded rationality, and organizational control theory, we derive eight propositions addressing the technical conditions of governance failure, the institutional dynamics that amplify it, and the mechanism design principles that constitute a viable alternative. We illustrate the propositions through the context of financial services agentic AI deployment, where the conditions are most advanced and the accountability stakes are highest. The paper contributes a theoretical extension of principal-agent theory to agentic AI contexts, a typology of governance failure mechanisms, and a set of testable propositions constituting a research agenda for empirical IS research on agentic AI governance.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6692058`

- title: Foundations of AI Security: Historical Evolution and Contemporary Threat Landscape
- authors: Rizwan Tanveer
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6692058
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Background. Artificial intelligence security has evolved from a peripheral concern within machine learning research into a central discipline of contemporary cybersecurity practice. The shift from static predictive models to autonomous agentic systems has introduced threat surfaces that legacy security frameworks were not designed to address, creating a widening gap between operational AI deployment and defensible security assurance. Purpose. This article traces the conceptual and technical evolution of AI security from early adversarial machine learning research to the contemporary threat landscape of 2026, examines the structural inadequacies of traditional cybersecurity frameworks when applied to AI systems, and positions emerging frameworks as essential instruments for practitioner-led AI security engineering. Approach. The article is structured as a narrative literature review drawing on authoritative primary sources, including NIST AI 100-2 E2025, NIST AI 600-1, the OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications (December 2025), the Cloud Security Alliance MAESTRO framework, MITRE ATLAS, and foundational adversarial machine learning literature spanning 2013 to 2025. Sources were selected for authority, currency, and direct relevance to operational AI security practice. Findings. Three structural shifts characterise the contemporary landscape: the expansion of attack surfaces from model parameters to data pipelines and agent ecosystems; the inadequacy of deterministic threat modelling frameworks for non-deterministic, learning systems; and the emergence of identity-centric and orchestration-centric security as the dominant paradigm for agentic AI. Implications. Practitioners require a layered framework approach, combining traditional cybersecurity controls with AI-specific governance instruments. The article provides a comparative basis for selecting and integrating these frameworks within enterprise security programmes.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6701438`

- title: Architectural Framework for Deterministic AI-Driven Design and Code Generation
- authors: Ravi Kumar Kappagantu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6701438
- keyword hits: ai agent

### abstract

Enterprise Architecture (EA) patterns-Circuit Breaker, Saga, Event Sourcing, CQRS-are the governance layer of software engineering. Yet they are stored as mutable wiki pages, informal Git commits, and unversioned PDFs, making them brittle inputs for AI code-generation agents. When two agents retrieve the Circuit Breaker pattern a month apart, they may receive substantively different specifications and produce incompatible implementations with no audit trace linking either artefact to a specific approved version. This paper proposes the EA-GKS framework-a rigorous, practically grounded generalization of the Governed Knowledge Source pattern ([9])-that treats EA patterns as immutable, versioned, composable, and provenance-tracked digital assets. We make five contributions: (1) An explicit mechanism-level mapping of all five GKS structural properties to the EA domain, with a formal extension delta capturing EA-specific additions; (2) A set-theoretic foundation including a quantifiable Determinism Coefficient D; (3) A worked Circuit Breaker case study with tri-pattern composition and end-to-end AI generation with constraint attestation; (4) A practical integration architecture for ArchiMate 3.1, Sparx Enterprise Architect, and LangGraph agents, with exception-handling protocols; (5) A prototype evaluation over 40 agent runs yielding D = 0.97 under EA-GKS versus D = 0.61 under unmanaged retrieval, 74% audit-effort reduction, and 100% false-composition prevention. Primary Claim: When EA patterns are stored and retrieved as Governed Knowledge Sources, AI agents produce near-deterministic, fully auditable design artefacts that satisfy TOGAF ADM governance gates, COBIT APO03, and EU AI Act Article 13 traceability requirements.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6754739`

- title: An interpretable few-shot load forecasting framework integrating feature interaction modeling and adaptive transfer learning
- authors: Yongchang Shen, Han Sun, Mingyang Ji
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6754739
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Under limited sample availability in the target region, conventional forecasting methods often struggle to fully capture complex interactions among multiple influencing factors, which hard to achieve high forecasting accuracy, and also face challenges in providing transparent explanations of the prediction process. Therefore, developing an effective method that simultaneously ensures forecasting accuracy, generalization capability, and model interpretability has become a critical issue in the fields of energy and power grid operation. To address this challenge, this study proposes a novel forecasting framework that integrates temporal feature interaction modeling, an adaptive transfer learning strategy, and explainable analysis, aiming to improve cross-regional load forecasting performance under few-shot conditions and provide transparent analytical support for power grid dispatch and management. Specifically, a feature selection algorithm is first employed to identify key explanatory variables, after which a PatchTST-STAR-BIGRU model based on feature interaction modeling is constructed. Furthermore, an adaptive transfer learning strategy based on the firefly algorithm is introduced to optimize the freezing and fine-tuning schemes across different encoding layers of the model. The experimental results show that the proposed model outperforms several benchmark models and achieves superior predictive performance using only a small number of target-domain samples. The Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) and the coefficient of determination (R²) reached 1.43% and 0.98, respectively. The model also demonstrated good stability and generalization ability in cross-seasonal testing. Finally, this study employs Shapley Additive exPlanations (SHAP) to interpret the contributions of key features and their interactions. The results revealed the dynamic influence mechanisms of historical load, electricity prices, and meteorological factors across different seasons. This further provides interpretable decision support for power system dispatch, operation, and demand-side management.

## uid: `arxiv:2605.23962v1`

- title: From Index to Equity: Pre-Training Transformers for Stock Return Prediction
- authors: Marie Soehl Coolsaet, Roberto Gallardo, Zhen Gao
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-12
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23962v1
- keyword hits: transformer model

### abstract

This research aims to leverage machine learning to improve stock price prediction and support informed investment decisions related to buying, selling, and holding assets. Specifically, this work investigates transformer-based models for stock prediction and examines the impact of pre-training strategies on forecasting performance. A transformer model was first pre-trained on the Toronto Stock Exchange Index (TSX) to predict intra-day return direction and subsequently fine-tuned on individual TSX stocks. The model was further adapted for return-value regression tasks. Performance was benchmarked against Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) and XGBoost models. Pre-training on the market index improved the binary cross-entropy loss for individual stock prediction from 0.69 to 0.64. The fine-tuned transformer regression model achieved lower mean squared error than the benchmark models, although the ensemble and XGBoost models achieved higher average daily returns. In addition, a practical application was developed to deliver real-time stock predictions for trading support. Future work will focus on increasing transformer model capacity, incorporating broader global technical indicators, and filtering out stocks with low predictability.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6758407`

- title: Dual-effect modification of steel slag by activated tailings for simultaneously improving its volume soundness and hydration reactivity
- authors: Qing Liu, Yanan Wu, Guofu Huang, Jun-xiang Wang, Haoyu Liu, Chenhao Jin, Weifeng Ren, Zhihao Li
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6758407
- keyword hits: prompting

### abstract

The utilization of steel slag (St) in construction materials is severely limited by its low hydration reactivity and poor volume soundness, primarily caused by the expansion of free lime (f-CaO). However, existing pretreatment technologies struggle to overcome both challenges simultaneously. This study proposes a novel dual-effect modification strategy for St, which involves using active Si species released from alkali-hydrothermally activated siliceous tailings (ATs) to drive the transformation of f-CaO and the construction of a porous calcium silicate hydrate (C-S-H) gel layer on the St surface. An “ATs-St” solid-liquid reaction system was established to achieve the SS modification, and it was found that that the f-CaO content in the modified St (MSt) was decreased to 0.83%, with an elimination rate of 83.95%. Le Chatelier volume soundness testing confirmed that MSt-blended cement, which an expansion value of 2.0 mm, exhibited superior volume soundness relative to St-blended cement, which had an expansion value of 5.0 mm. Concurrently, a pozzolanic reaction between Si species from ATs and Ca-bearing phases (e.g., f-CaO, C3S, and C2S) in St formed a highly porous C-S-H gel layer on the St surface. This porous C-S-H network acted as nucleation sites, significantly accelerating the cement hydration, prompting the production of more hydration products, and resulting in a denser microstructure. Consequently, the 3-day and 28-day activity indices of MSt reached 81.17% and 99.04%, respectively, corresponding to increases of 43.92% and 35.19%, compared with those of raw St.
