## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7033079`

- title: Collateral Psychosis: AI Surveillance Infrastructure as an Etiological and Iatrogenic Factor in Paranoia-Spectrum Conditions
- authors: Travis Gilly
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7033079
- keyword hits: large language model

### abstract

The emerging literature on AI-induced psychosis documents harm arising exclusively from direct interaction between users and large language model systems: a user prompts the AI, the AI responds, and the conversational loop escalates into delusional states, emotional dependency, or reality dismantlement. This paper argues that the field's exclusive focus on interaction-based harm has obscured a categorically distinct phenomenon: AI-induced psychosis in the complete absence of user interaction. AI systems deployed as surveillance infrastructure (automated license plate readers, geofence analytics, facial recognition, behavioral inference engines) can generate and sustain paranoia-spectrum conditions in individuals who never open a chat window, never prompt a model, and never receive a generated response. The mechanism operates through environmental exposure rather than conversational engagement, following the etiological model of trauma-induced and environmentally-induced psychosis rather than the substance-induced model that governs interaction-based cases. Drawing on established psychiatric epidemiology of environmental risk factors for psychosis, independently documented cases of AI systems inducing psychotic states in previously healthy individuals, and a clinical analysis of surveillance-induced therapeutic destruction, this paper introduces the category of collateral psychosis, defined as clinically significant paranoia-spectrum symptomatology arising from ambient exposure to AI-powered infrastructure rather than from direct engagement with AI systems. The paper identifies two population-level effects: an iatrogenic effect, in which surveillance infrastructure degrades the treatability of existing paranoia-spectrum conditions by eliminating the falsifiability of persecutory beliefs; and an etiological effect, in which sustained exposure to confirmed, inescapable surveillance produces new-onset paranoia-spectrum symptomatology in individuals with no prior psychiatric history. The distinction between these effects has implications for clinical intervention, legal liability, and the scope of the AI safety research program.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7083040`

- title: From Learning Dilemmas to Institutional Guidance: A Qualitative Study of Students’ Experiences with Generative AI in Higher Education
- authors: OYA AYDIN
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7083040
- keyword hits: generative ai, generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

This study examines how students experience and interpret the use of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) in higher education. Adopting a qualitative, exploratory design, data were collected from nineteen undergraduate tourism students and analyzed using qualitative content analysis supported by code frequency and co-occurrence patterns. The findings reveal a multidimensional structure of student perceptions. In the learning process, GenAI is experienced through a benefit–concern dynamic, simultaneously enhancing efficiency, supporting learning, and providing rapid access to information while also raising concerns about reduced cognitive effort, dependency, and diminished self-efficacy. In ethical terms, students draw clear boundaries between acceptable supportive use and unacceptable substitution of their own work. Traditional assessment methods are widely perceived as inadequate, leading to a strong preference for process-oriented and interactive approaches. Furthermore, students express a strong demand for clear and consistent institutional policies that guide, rather than prohibit, AI use. The study concludes that balanced, pedagogically guided integration is essential to address the opportunities and risks associated with GenAI in higher education.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7059539`

- title: The Anatomy of Censorship and Propaganda: Evidence from Russian Wikipedias
- authors: Ekaterina Zhuravskaya, Ulrich Matter, Ruben Durante, Vladimir Avetian
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7059539
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Authoritarian regimes shape citizens' knowledge and beliefs through censorship and propaganda. We study which narratives they suppress and promote using a rare natural experiment: Ruwiki, created in 2023 by cloning Russian-language Wikipedia and selectively rewriting its 1.9 million articles under regime-aligned editorial control. We compare the two platforms twenty months after the fork, using within-Wikipedia changes as a placebo benchmark. We find that Ruwiki's editing patterns resemble professional rather than volunteer-driven content production. An LLM-based comparison and machine-learning analyses of text deletions and insertions reveal a coherent ideology organized around three pillars: Russia's image vis-àvis the rest of the world, domestic politics, and culture. The resulting narratives legitimize Russia's past and present acts of aggression, suppress accounts of domestic opposition and human-rights violations, and promote traditional values. Culture's centrality reveals how authoritarian information control penetrates domains far beyond formal politics, including morality, gender, family life, law, and psychology. Article deletions are rare and confined to topics that are difficult to reframe, while editing is broad, consistent with a preference for reframing existing content rather than leaving unanswered queries: 27% of articles undergo at least some change, 2.8% are heavily edited, and only 0.14% are deleted. Rewriting sensitive topics reduces readability, suggesting that ideological control is costly. An external benchmark using official high-school history textbooks confirms that Ruwiki's revisions move articles on modern history toward state-sanctioned narratives. The results show how authoritarian information control constructs a regime-aligned account of the world and Russia's place in it.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7078663`

- title: Pseudo-Absence Design and Cross-Validation Govern Land-Cover Habitat-Model Accuracy and Scale of Effect: A Three-Region Benchmark for the Asian Badger (Meles leucurus)
- authors: Jeong-Cheol Kim, Sunmin Lee
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7078663
- keyword hits: foundation model

### abstract

Ecologists routinely report near-perfect discrimination in land-cover habitat models (AUC > 0.95), yet such figures rarely translate to field-validated predictions. Using the Asian badger (Meles leucurus), a common Korean forest mustelid with well-understood ecological requirements, we show that inflated accuracy stems from two analyst decisions made before modelling begins: pseudo-absence design and cross-validation scheme. Absences restricted to built-up land created a trivially separable forest-versus-urban contrast; all models—including a deep convolutional neural network—reached AUC ≈ 0.97–1.00. Replacing built-up absences with ecologically valid random background and substituting spatial block for random k-fold cross-validation collapsed AUC to ≈0.80–0.82 and reversed the inferred optimal analysis scale from home-range-sized (≈6 km) to fine (≈390 m). A virtual-species experiment confirmed this reversal is causal: the corrected protocol recovered a deliberately broad true scale, whereas the biased protocol always returned the finest kernel regardless of ground truth. The pattern recurred in two further counties of the same province, though the optimal scale shifted between them. Under the corrected protocol, Gaussian distance-weighted kernels outperformed box windows, individual land-cover classes each had a distinct characteristic scale, and multi-seasonal Sentinel-2 and terrain covariates lifted AUC to 0.85–0.91. Added model complexity did not help: convolutional networks trained from scratch trailed logistic regression at ≈330 presences, while a pretrained tabular foundation model (TabPFN) sharpened rank-discrimination (AUC) but not calibration (Boyce index)—a reminder that the best model is itself metric-dependent. We recommend ecologically valid background, spatial block cross-validation, and calibration-aware evaluation as minimum prerequisites for credible scale-of-effect inference from land-cover habitat model

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7080180`

- title: The Physical Limits of Compute: Dynamic Risk Contagion Networks Between Semiconductor and Energy Infrastructure Assets Under the AI Super Cycle
- authors: Fuli Yang, Shiyu Lin
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7080180
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

As generative AI transitions into large-scale commercialization and hyperscale data center construction, the binding constraint on compute expansion has shifted materially from chip supply to power availability. Using daily return data from January 2023 to June 2026 (N=874), this paper employs a DCC-GARCH(1,1) framework to examine the time-varying co-movement between semiconductor assets (SOXX) and energy infrastructure assets (URA-global uranium/nuclear, and XLU-utilities). The results reveal a pronounced structural regime shift: the dynamic conditional correlation between SOXX and URA averaged just 0.284 during the first eighteen months of the sample, but rose to 0.518 in the post-2024H2 period-a near-doubling that coincides with high-profile announcements of nuclear power restarts and private grid construction by major technology firms. A two-sample t-test confirms this shift is statistically significant (t=3.395, p<0.001). Individual GARCH(1,1)-t estimates document strong volatility persistence across all three assets (α+β≈0.95-1.00), and sub-period analysis shows that the SOXX-XLU correlation also widened from near-zero (0.006) to 0.139 over the same horizon. These findings document an emerging physical-financial nexus: AI mania is crossing the boundary of the virtual economy to generate measurable volatility co-movement in the physical infrastructure of energy supply. The results carry direct implications for cross-asset allocation, factor model construction, and systemic risk monitoring.

- title: An Evaluation-Driven Iterative Refinement Framework forHuman-in-the-Loop EV Charging and Routing Solvers
- authors: Usman Zafar, Sertac Bayhan, Abdullah Abdul Jabbar, Raka Jovanovic
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7081500
- keyword hits: large language model, llm

### abstract

The integration of AI-enabled automation into electric vehicle (EV) infrastructure enables domain ex-perts with limited programming expertise to develop complex charging and routing solvers. We presentan evaluation-driven, human-in-the-loop framework in which domain experts specify high-level physicalcriteria and initial prompts, while a Large Language Model (LLM) agent autonomously generates, tests,and repairs solver code through iterative refinement. An evaluator executes candidate code and providesdetailed failure evidence—including criterion-level mismatches, execution traces, and feedback from an LLM-based judge—to guide prompt updates. We demonstrate this workflow on four industrial benchmarks: twosmart routing tasks (synthetic and real Doha road networks) and two smart charging tasks (synthetic andsimulator-based). Ablation and prompt-sensitivity studies on the calibration set show that both diagnostictraces and iterative refinement are critical for reliable convergence. The complete feedback configurationconsistently converges on all development split benchmarks, achieving up to 100% exact-match successon routing and 80.0–84.4% on charging (with judge-accepted tie-breaks), compared to 0–80% for one-shotbaselines. On the test split, our framework attains 80–100% exact-match success, with 100% of outputsmeeting domain-specific acceptance criteria under manual inspection. These results demonstrate that ex-plainable, criterion-grounded feedback loops support effective human-in-the-loop algorithm development,enabling engineers to retain authority over specifications while delegating code exploration and repair to AIagents.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7077838`

- title: Cognitive Parsing Network for Action Quality Assessment
- authors: Junyang Li, Yi Tian, Keyi Huang, Yi Jin, Ziyi Huang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-08
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7077838
- keyword hits: llm, llms

### abstract

Action quality assessment (AQA) aims to quantify how well an action is performed. Existing methods typically divide videos into uniform temporal segments for feature extraction, directly followed by naive feature fusion and score regression. This easily results in fragmented action semantics and insensitivity to critical technical nodes. In this paper, we propose a novel Cognitive Parsing Network (CoPNet), inspired by the human judges’ cognitive process of ’Decomposition and Aggregation’. CoPNet consists of a Semantics-driven Decomposition Module (SdDM) and a Contribution-aware Aggregation Module (CaAM). The SdDM parses the whole video as a series of semantic-aware embeddings corresponding to various evaluable sub-actions. The CaAM aggregates them to a scorerelated feature according to their contributions to the final performance. In both modules, we take fulladvantage of the professional knowledge underlying pre-trained LLMs to provide reliable guidance to them. Therefore, our CoPNet not only ensures the semantic coherence and integrity of the whole action, but also is discriminative to the crucial and essential scoring clues. Extensive experiments on FineDiving and MTL-AQA datasets demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.
