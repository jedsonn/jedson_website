## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6558458`

- title: Interface Bifurcation in AI-Mediated Service Ecosystems: A Framework for Classifying Service Architectures and Their Strategic Implications in the Agentic Economy
- authors: MyungWoon Oh
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6558458
- keyword hits: agentic, ai agent

### abstract

The proliferation of autonomous AI agents as service consumers challenges foundational assumptions in service management and platform economics. Existing frameworks-including Service-Dominant Logic and two-sided market theory-presuppose human actors as primary service beneficiaries, a condition being systematically eroded by agentic AI adoption. This paper introduces Interface Bifurcation Theory (IBT), a conceptual-typological framework classifying digital services along two dimensions: AI Consumption Intensity (ACI) and Human Visual Oversight Necessity (HVON). The resulting 2×2 typology identifies four service architectures-Traditional Visual, Supervised AI, Conversational, and Headless AI-each with distinct competitive logic and investment priorities. IBT introduces three novel constructs: Interface Value Migration, the Dual Architecture Burden, and Ecosystem Externality, and derives eight empirically testable propositions. Four illustrative cases-Bloomberg, Stripe, Epic Systems, and Perplexity AI-provide empirical grounding. Implications for service management theory, platform economics, and digital strategy are discussed.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6682018`

- title: Fiduciary Architecture for AI Systems: Power Needs Duties
- authors: Samantha King
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6682018
- keyword hits: chatgpt

### abstract

Most governance frameworks for artificial intelligence ask what AI systems do: how they perform, where they fail, what they disclose. These are necessary questions. They are not sufficient ones. This paper advances a different frame. As AI systems increasingly mediate access, shape judgment, and function as dependency infrastructure for individuals and institutions, a foundational governance question has emerged: what do these systems owe the people who rely on them? That is a fiduciary question. We have barely begun to ask it seriously. Fiduciary Architecture is a governance design model that imposes duty structures on AI deployments proportionate to the asymmetries of power, dependency, and influence they create. This paper introduces eight core duties, a tiered application framework scaled to consequence level, a failure-mode taxonomy, and a structural mapping to the GICD Framework as the operational assurance layer. Its argument is that without enforceable duty structures, AI governance produces compliance theater rather than institutional accountability. AI safety asks what systems do. Fiduciary Architecture asks what they owe. Those are different questions. They lead to different designs. A note on methodology: this paper was developed in active collaboration with ChatGPT (OpenAI). The framework, analysis, and conclusions are the author's. The collaboration was used for structural development and drafting. No undisclosed optimization shaped its outputs. This disclosure is consistent with the Duty of Cognitive Integrity the paper itself proposes.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6682238`

- title: Tag-Conditioned Retrieval-Augmented Commentary Assistance in Replay-Aligned StarCraft: Brood War Segments
- authors: Doyoung Kim, Ho-Taek Joo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6682238
- keyword hits: fine-tuning, large language model, retrieval-augmented

### abstract

Expert commentary in StarCraft: Brood War requires not only fluent language generation but also the selection of strategically relevant evidence and the expression of that evidence in a discourse form appropriate to the current broadcast segment. In this paper, we study this problem as an offline replay-aligned commentary-assistance task under a controlled protocol with oracle segment boundaries and reference discourse tags. Building on CASTER, a replay-aligned corpus of professional SC1 broadcasts, we formulate commentary assistance as a case-based retrieval-and-generation problem in which discourse-compatible historical cases are retrieved from a non-test pool and used to guide generation for the current segment. To improve cross-view retrieval from replay-derived context to commentary-relevant cases, we train a lightweight replay-centered encoder by distilling commentary-side relational structure. At inference time, retrieved replay-context–commentary pairs are filtered by discourse tag and supplied as in-context demonstrations to a large language model. Experiments on match-disjoint splits with four generators show that tagconditioned retrieval improves over zero-shot generation across all frozen generators and generally outperforms unconditioned retrieval. For example, with GPT-OSS-120B, tag-conditioned retrieval improves BLEU-4 from 3.50 to 5.72 and METEOR from 11.23 to 12.18 relative to zero-shot generation. When combined with supervised fine-tuning, retrieval remains complementary and yields competitive or best results on most reported metrics. These findings support discourse-compatible historical-case retrieval as an effective design choice for offline segment-level commentary assistance in dynamic, partially observable environments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6582598`

- title: Synthetic Test Data Generation Using Agentic AI
- authors: Gayathri Surianarayanan
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-04-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6582598
- keyword hits: agentic, ai agent

### abstract

The growing trend of artificial intelligence (AI) being implemented in various fields-including regulated, safety-critical, and those involving significant amounts of datahas produced an acute need for large quantities of quality test data. Use of actual data from production for testing purposes is typically restricted due to factors such as data privacy laws, protection of intellectual property rights, ethical considerations, and the potential operational risks associated with the use of sensitive data. As an alternative, there have been numerous attempts to produce synthetic data; however, previous methods used to create synthetic data are generally unable to provide contextually relevant data, dynamically adapt to changing system requirements, and be semantically aligned with the characteristics of actual data in use in the system. This paper proposes the development of an Agentic AI-based framework for the generation of synthetic test data, which includes autonomous, goal-oriented AI agents that collaborate to develop, produce, validate, and improve the synthetic test data. In contrast to the static nature of typical synthetic data generation methods (including rule-based methodologies and generative algorithms), this proposed framework utilizes multiple agent types-including schema agents, constraint agents, scenario agents, adversarial agents, and validation agents-to dynamically evaluate the structure and content of the synthetic test data generated during each iteration. This allows for the continuous evolution of the test data based on changes in the system under test, regulatory restrictions, and the behavior of the models. Through empirical testing and analysis across both enterprise and AI-enabled system testing environments, the proposed framework was able to demonstrate better test data coverage, higher defect discovery rates, stronger privacy guarantees, and increased robustness testing when compared to the conventional synthetic data generation pipelines. Therefore, the proposed method represents a viable option for generating synthetic test data at scale while providing auditable and regulatory compliance solutions for AIdriven systems.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6691228`

- title: Accomplishing Authorship with GenAI
- authors: Robert Braun
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6691228
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

This commentary develops a phenomenological account of authorship in scholarly life under conditions increasingly mediated by generative AI. Rather than treating authorship as the expression of an isolated interior subject, it approaches it as a fragile social accomplishment through which a text becomes accountable, recognizable, and institutionally authorized as scholarship. Drawing on ethnomethodology, institutional ethnography, and Science and Technology Studies, the paper argues that generative AI does not simply informs authorship from the outside but renders newly visible the situated labour by which scholarly writing is assembled, repaired, authorized, and made accountable. The central issue is not whether AI can be an author, but how the relation between textual production and human responsibility is being reconfigured. As GenAI enters both writing and evaluative infrastructures, authorship emerges as a contested site of practical, institutional, and normative negotiation. The paper concludes by proposing a reflexive CRediT-AI framework to disclose mediation without dissolving human accountability.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6682498`

- title: Generative AI and Investment Research: Evidence from Analyst Reports
- authors: Shawn X. Huang, Artur Hugon, Tengfei Zhang, Wenting Zheng
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6682498
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

This study provides large-sample evidence on generative AI (GenAI) adoption by equity analysts and its impact on investment research quality. Using a supervised machine learning model to detect AI-assisted content, we find that such reports are associated with greater earnings forecast accuracy. This accuracy benefit is more pronounced for analysts facing greater research demands and those with lower baseline skills. To establish causality, we leverage an exogenous shock to analyst workload from overlapping earnings calls, and find that AI usage is a significant driver of improved forecast accuracy. We also find that forecasts based on AI-assisted reports trigger stronger capital market reactions, indicating that investors perceive such investment research as more informative. Content analysis of analyst reports reveals that GenAI empowers analysts beyond simple text drafting: AI-assisted reports employ more diverse valuation models, discuss a broader range of financial topics and stakeholders, and exhibit more structured reasoning patterns characterized by analytical logic and causal explanations. Overall, our findings suggest that generative AI enhances the fundamental capabilities of information intermediaries in capital markets.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6691889`

- title: Leveraging Generative AI in the Development, Adaptation, and Evaluation of EAP Materials
- authors: Maimoona Sayeed
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6691889
- keyword hits: chatgpt, generative ai

### abstract

This study explores teacher and student perspectives on the use of Generative AI in the development, adaptation, and evaluation of English for Academic Purposes (EAP) materials. Using a mixed-method, exploratory design, data were collected through questionnaires, interviews, focus groups, and analysis of AI-generated materials. The findings reveal that while both teachers and students recognise the efficiency and accessibility of AI tools such as ChatGPT, their perceptions differ significantly. Teachers emphasised accuracy, pedagogical value, and ethical concerns, whereas students prioritise usability and convenience. AI was found to enhance engagement and support material development but also posed risks of over-reliance and reduced critical engagement. The study highlights the need for structured integration, teacher training, and digital literacy development. It concludes that AI can be an effective support tool when balanced with human judgment, promoting meaningful learning while maintaining academic integrity and learner autonomy.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6578940`

- title: Visual Regression Testing at Scale: A Computer Vision and AI-Driven Framework for Enterprise UI Validation
- authors: Rajeshkumar Rajaseakaran Nair
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6578940
- keyword hits: llm

### abstract

Visual regression testing remains a critical yet underexplored challenge in enterprise software quality assurance. Traditional pixel-based comparison tools frequently generate excessive false positives and fail to adapt dynamically to evolving UI components at scale. This paper proposes a comprehensive AI-driven visual regression testing framework that integrates Computer Vision (CV) techniques-including convolutional neural network (CNN)-based image analysis, perceptual hashing, and intelligent baseline management-to enable scalable, accurate UI validation across complex enterprise applications. The framework addresses core limitations of existing commercial tools such as Applitools, Percy, and Screener by introducing a multi-layered detection architecture that separates semantic changes from stylistic noise. We examine architectural patterns for integrating visual intelligence into CI/CD pipelines, discuss key challenges such as dynamic content handling, cross-resolution inconsistencies, and flaky test management, and present empirical observations on failure classification drawn from large-scale enterprise deployments. The proposed framework is designed to substantially improve defect detection precision while reducing false positive rates compared to conventional pixel-diff methods. Our analysis suggests that combining deep learning-based image comparison with contextual semantic understanding can significantly reduce maintenance overhead while improving defect detection accuracy in large-scale enterprise environments. The framework is designed to be extensible for accessibility validation, multimodal LLM integration, and autonomous self-healing baseline management.
