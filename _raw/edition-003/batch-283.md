## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6759411`

- title: Mind the Decision Void: A Systematic Literature Review of Generative AI Integration in Management Education
- authors: Suvodip Sen
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6759411
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

Artificial Intelligence (AI), or Generative AI, is revolutionizing the corporate world, and AI literacy must be part of management education to develop future leaders. This systematic literature review explores this integration by synthesizing existing research with a combined Antecedents-Decisions-Outcomes (ADO) and Theory-Context-Method (TCM) framework. A total of 174 peer-reviewed articles from Scopus and Web of Science were analyzed following PRISMA guidelines. Results indicate a rapidly growing but structurally unbalanced body of knowledge. Specifically, this systematic review finds 277 associations in total (208 positive, 43 neutral, and 26 negative) across the antecedent, decision, and outcome mapping. While technological antecedents and productivity-oriented outcomes are heavily theorized, there is a critical “decision void”: the literature does not offer much explanation of how AI is actually embedded into pedagogical practices and curriculum design. Furthermore, research is very learner-centered and quantitative, often ignoring the institutional and faculty perspectives. This study contributes significant value by demonstrating the disconnect between the intention to adopt technology and the real transformation of education. It offers actionable implications for educators and policymakers to move from passive AI adoption to active and meaningful integration and provides a comprehensive agenda for future context-sensitive, process-oriented research.

- title: Industrial Multi-AI Agent Collaborative System based on Large-Small Model Fusion and Retrieval-Augmented Generation
- authors: Xiao Lai, Han Wang, Min Liu, Li Bai
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6756498
- keyword hits: ai agent, retrieval-augmented

### abstract

With the rapid advancement of intelligent manufacturing, industrial systems generate vast amounts of unstructured data, yet face significant challenges in data utilization, knowledge retrieval accuracy, decision-making efficiency, and human-machine collaboration. To address these issues, this paper proposes a large-small model fusion and retrieval-augmented generation based industrial multi-AI agent collaborative system (IMACS). First, a RAG-driven data governance pipeline is introduced, featuring IndDocMaster (a high-precision unstructured data parsing model) enabling end-to-end document understanding through multi-source fusion and a data flywheel mechanism, significantly enhancing knowledge extraction quality. Second, a hierarchical agent architecture from L1 to L4 is designed, achieving cognitive-execution synergy via large-model orchestration and small-model execution. Finally, an Agent-Human-Cyber-Physical System (A-HCPS) framework is established to enable autonomous, collaborative decision-making. Experimental results show that the proposed system outperforms existing methods in industrial data parsing, knowledge retrieval, and near-expert-level maintenance decision-making with over 60% faster response, demonstrating its effectiveness in advancing industrial intelligence.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6761351`

- title: Trustworthy multistakeholder demand forecasting for highspeed trains: A dualbranch architecture integrating TimeLLM and MCHMM
- authors: Jinping Li, Guofu Ding, Rong Li, Shengfeng Qin, Haizhu Zhang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6761351
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

User demand forecasting for complex products faces multiple challenges including dynamic evolution, multiscale fluctuations, and insufficient prediction reliability. Moreover, practical applications are often constrained by ultrasmallsample sizes. Although existing timeseries forecasting methods based on large language models (e.g., TimeLLM) can leverage pretrained knowledge to capture global dependencies, they lack adaptive modeling of demand evolution stages and cannot quantify prediction uncertainty, thereby limiting their applicability in risksensitive decisionmaking. To address these limitations, this paper proposes an enhanced Multiscale Coupled Hidden Markov Model (MCHMM) and integrates it with TimeLLM to establish a dualbranch collaborative forecasting architecture. In this framework, the TimeLLM branch performs deep temporal representation learning, while the MCHMM branch explicitly characterizes interlayer and intralayer coupling relationships and evolution patterns among multiple stakeholder demands through adaptive state number learning, multiscale feature extraction, memoryenhanced state updating, and endtoend uncertainty quantification. The two branches are dynamically fused via a learnable fusion network, achieving synergistic integration of structured priors and datadriven insights. Experimental validation on multiple realworld user demand datasets (including an ultrasmallsample scenario with 7 samples) demonstrates that compared to the baseline TimeLLM, the proposed method reduces the test MSE by 1.46–1.98%, narrows the generalization gap by 78% (from 0.002971 to 0.000656), and provides wellcalibrated uncertainty estimates that correlate strongly with prediction errors. These results validate the effectiveness of the proposed dualbranch architecture for robust, uncertaintyaware demand forecasting in datasparse industrial environments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6714238`

- title: The Irrecoverable Institution - Why Replayability - Not Explainability - Is the Governance Standard for Agentic Banking
- authors: Deepak Aggarwal
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6714238
- keyword hits: agentic, ai agent

### abstract

Financial institutions have accelerated the deployment of Agentic AI systems. These systems have the capabilities to make decisions and are operating faster than any governance mechanism can respond. However, when such decisions go wrong, the biggest cost incurred is not due to their very first mistake, but because the financial institution lacks the capability of replaying and governing the decision path until other costs increase significantly. The objective of this paper is to propose Decision Replay as the base governance framework for agentic financial institutions, which would be the capability to replay and govern decisions at any moment, via any number of AI agents, back to the policies and authorization behind such a decision. While the explainability framework (LIME, SHAP, and interpretable model) provides information as to how and why a certain model came up with that decision, the question about whether it was a properly governed, trackable, and authorized process cannot be answered with explainability frameworks. Four different case studies of failures are explored in this paper: Silicon Valley Bank (March 2023), Knight Capital Group (August 2012), the "Gilt" market collapse in the UK (September 2022), and the credit agentic decision failure . In each of the four failures studied above, 35% to 60% of the firm's losses are a direct result of failing to track, audit, and act on decisions. EU AI Act, DORA, MAS FEAT, and Basel guidance are examined. The paper builds on previous work on the Decision Integrity Chain™ (DIC™)- an eight-layer governance framework and introduces the FUSE/STAGE architectural stack that operationalizes it.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6760803`

- title: HILIPS: A Human-In-the-Loop Image Labeling Pipeline for Visual-Semantic Ambiguity
- authors: Taehyung Noh, Beejin Son, Junghyun Kim, Haein Yeo, Kyungsik Han
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6760803
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

Image labeling in industrial environments frequently encounters visual-semantic ambiguity, where visually similar objects cannot be resolved from appearance alone. We present HILIPS (Human-In-the-Loop Image Labeling Pipeline for Semantic ambiguity), a three-stage framework that integrates multimodal large language models (MLLMs) with interactive segmentation and lightweight object detection. In the Cold-start Labeling stage, an MLLM and SAM2 generate initial labels without the requirement for training data. The Label Transfer stage distills MLLM outputs into a deployable YOLO model for efficient local inference. In the Iterative Refinement stage, automation is progressively scaled through a confidence-based review. A user study involving 10 participants showed consistent reductions in perceived workload (NASA-TLX) and labeling time, alongside improved system usability (SUS) as the level of automation increased. HILIPS is released as open-source software with a modular architecture that allows for independent replacement of MLLM, segmentation models, and detection components to suit diverse industrial requirements.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6758116`

- title: A Conditional Diffusion-Based Generative AI Model for Industrial Predictive Analytics
- authors: Ko Donghyun, Xiaolei Fang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-13
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6758116
- keyword hits: generative ai

### abstract

Accurate prediction of remaining useful life (RUL) or time-to-failure (TTF) from degradation signals is a central problem in prognostics and health management across domains such as aerospace, manufacturing, and energy. Existing approaches largely fall into two categories: model-based methods that explicitly model degradation trajectories under parametric assumptions, and data-driven methods that directly map observed signals to failure time via dimensionality reduction and regression. Representative examples of these approaches considered in this paper include Bayesian updating and PCA-based lognormal regression. While effective for smooth and monotonic degradation patterns, both paradigms often degrade when applied to irregular, nonstationary, or multimodal degradation processes commonly encountered in real-world environments. To address these limitations, we propose a conditional diffusion–based generative framework that learns the full conditional distribution of future degradation trajectories given partial observations, enabling flexible and probabilistic TTF prediction. By modeling the data distribution directly, diffusion models can approximate nonlinear, heavy-tailed, and multimodal degradation behaviors. To rigorously evaluate performance, we construct synthetic degradation datasets with controlled increases in structural complexity, incorporating Brownian-motion noise, localized perturbations, and multi-regime dynamics. Extensive benchmarks against Bayesian updating and PCA-based lognormal regression demonstrate that the proposed diffusion model consistently achieves lower error under complex settings, particularly near failure thresholds and under complex, heteroscedastic conditions. These results position diffusion model as a robust and scalable alternative for predictive analytics in realistic prognostic.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6736563`

- title: Zero-Shot Meets ZeroHedge: Multi-Dimensional LLM Sentiment Decomposition of Contrarian Media for VIX Prediction
- authors: Yan Sun
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-14
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6736563
- keyword hits: llama, llm

### abstract

Text-based financial forecasting often compresses narratives into a single sentiment score or non-directional topic weights, obscuring economically distinct channels relevant for volatility prediction. We propose a structured large-language-model framework for VIX forecasting from editorially biased alternative financial media. Using LLaMA 3 in a zero-shot setting, we score 25,137 ZeroHedge articles from 2021 to 2025 along three theory-driven dimensions: macroeconomic uncertainty, institutional action, and risk-off intensity. A bias-correcting prompt separates factual financial content from the outlet's persistently bearish editorial tone. Daily text scores are combined with lagged VIX history in a late-fusion LSTM for VIX prediction. Results are strongest at medium-term horizons, where textual information is most valuable. At the 8-day horizon, the macroeconomic uncertainty dimension achieves a PR-AUC of 0.708, compared with 0.695 for HAR(3), 0.688 for a VIX-only LSTM, 0.620 for FinBERT-Sentiment, and 0.681 for a same-family single-factor LLaMA baseline. Removing the editorially noisy risk-off dimension further improves performance to 0.713 at the 9-day horizon. An illustrative risk-alert overlay flags elevated-risk days followed, on average, by a 2.88-point higher 9-day VIX change than non-flagged days. These findings indicate that, for sentiment-driven VIX forecasting, structured decomposition, not model capability alone, drives the predictive gain. The study also releases a 1,285-day multi-dimensional ZeroHedge score panel to support replication and research on economically driven VIX forecasting.

- title: An Automated OPC UA Information Modeling Method Integrating Large Language Models and Static Verification
- authors: Yuge Yan, Gongwu Sun, Peng Zheng, Danhong Zhang, Yinxin Su
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-14
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6755354
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

OPC UA provides a unified semantic framework for industrial information models. However, existing OPC UA information modeling is still largely performed manually, which is time-consuming and labor-intensive, especially for complex industrial diagnostic logic. This paper proposes an automated OPC UA modeling method for industrial diagnostic source code that integrates large language models with static verification. The method takes diagnostic source code as input, uses a large language model to extract candidate diagnostic semantics, and applies static verification to remove duplicate nodes, score evidence, and correct data types, finally generating an XML information model compliant with the OPC UA specification. Experiments on UDP, CAN, and RS485 diagnostic code show that the method effectively extracts key diagnostic semantics and generates OPC UA information models with reasonable structure and good robustness.
