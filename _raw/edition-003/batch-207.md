## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6987086`

- title: CoPAMoE: Cognitive Process-Aware Mixture of Experts for Bias Understanding and Mitigation
- authors: Xingshen Liu, Fuxin Wang, Fan Wang, Zhixuan Jia, Haowei Wang, Congjing Ran
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-24
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6987086
- keyword hits: fine-tuning, llm, llms

### abstract

Bias handling in expert-augmented LLMs is not merely a matter of routing tasks to experts, it requires coordinating how biased cues are detected, how their social meanings are attributed, and how interventions are generated. However, existing parameter-efficient fine-tuning and mixture-of-experts approaches typically organize specialization around task labels, domains, or input-level routing signals, rather than explicitly modeling how evidence of bias should inform its interpretation and subsequent rewriting. This paper proposes CoPAMoE, a cognitive process-aware PEFT-MoE framework for bias understanding and mitigation. CoPAMoE organizes bias-related subtasks into three functional stages, Cue Perception, Attribution Reasoning, and Intervention Control. It combines stage-partitioned LoRA experts, shared experts, a task--stage routing prior, and an Adjacent Stage Gated Coordination (ASGC) module. The resulting architecture explicitly separates stage-specific adaptation from task-general bias signals and supports controlled information transfer between adjacent stages. Experiments on a unified benchmark built from CrowS-Pairs, StereoSet, and WinoBias show that CoPAMoE improves average performance over representative PEFT and MoE baselines on two backbone LLMs. Ablation, routing, representation, oracle, and efficiency analyses further suggest that the gains are associated with stage-aware specialization and cross-stage dependency transfer.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6989411`

- title: Knowledge Graph-Enhanced Carbon Impact Assessment of Construction and Demolition Waste with Large Language Model-Based Decision Support
- authors: Yu Gao, Tak Wing (Kenneth) Yiu, Xuesong Shen, Vivian Tam
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-24
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6989411
- keyword hits: large language model, retrieval-augmented

### abstract

Construction and demolition waste sector generates substantial carbon emissions across sorting, transportation, and disposal phases. However, existing assessment approaches often generate static numerical results that offer limited traceability, regulatory interpretation, and decision-support capability. This study proposes an integrated framework that combines a knowledge graph with a large language model for construction and demolition waste carbon emission management. A domain-specific construction and demolition waste Carbon Knowledge Graph is constructed in Neo4j to integrate waste categories, treatment methods, facility information, source-referenced emission factors, and regulatory constraints. Carbon emissions from sorting, transportation, and disposal are quantified using an emission factor-based approach and embedded into the graph for traceable accounting. A retrieval-augmented generation mechanism connects the knowledge graph with a large language model, enabling natural language querying and evidence-based explanations. A residential demolition case study demonstrates the applicability of the framework. The results indicate that transportation is the dominant emission phase, accounting for 63.6% of total emissions, and show that the recycling-based pathway for concrete waste reduces emissions by 36.46% compared with the landfill-only baseline. This study contributes to bridging knowledge management and carbon modeling, providing an explainable, traceable, and regulation-aware decision-support approach for low-carbon construction and demolition waste management.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6989759`

- title: Beyond Paradigms: RMKO Framework for Job Recommender Systems from Classical to Agentic Architectures
- authors: arbiya ochi
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-24
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6989759
- keyword hits: agentic, llm

### abstract

Job Recommender Systems (JRS) have evolved from classical keyword-matching archi- tectures into sophisticated agentic platforms that increasingly mediate high-stakes hiring and career decisions. Existing surveys remain anchored in paradigm-centric taxonomies (collaborative filtering, content-based, knowledge-based, hybrid, deep learning) that fail to capture the modular, multidimensional nature of contemporary LLM-RAG-agentic architectures. To address these limitations, we introduce the RMKO framework a novel four- dimensional compositional taxonomy (Representation (R), Matching (M), Knowledge Integration (K), and Output & Interaction (O)). this paper presents a systematic sur- vey to comprehensively cover JRS evolution from 2011 to 2025, explicitly including the emerging LLM-RAG-agentic era. Following a PRISMA-inspired protocol, we analyse 88 primary studies through multidimensional coding, Spearman correlations, and Hamming- distance clustering. The analysis reveals differential progress across dimensions, persis- tent asymmetries in knowledge grounding and output sophistication, and four emergent architectural archetypes with accelerating convergence toward Interactive/Agentic Gen- erative systems. The RMKO framework provides both a diagnostic lens and a practical roadmap for designing more trustworthy, explainable, and equitable employment AI, together with a prioritised agenda of future research directions.​

## uid: `arxiv:2606.25484v1`

- title: From Causal Discovery to Implementation: An Agentic AI Framework for E-Scooter Mobility Hub Planning Across 29 German Cities
- authors: Meng Jin, Melanie Handrich, Simone Martinenz, Nicholas Hoeser, Ziyue Li
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-24
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25484v1
- keyword hits: agentic, large language model, llm

### abstract

Existing approaches to e-scooter mobility hub planning lack city-type-specific causal evidence. Demand models are typically correlational, built on proprietary trip data, and do not distinguish how driver profiles vary across urban typologies. This paper presents a three-phase agentic AI framework that constructs a Causal Template Library from public GBFS data across 29 German cities, encoding which environmental features causally drive hotspot demand for each combination of city type (large, university, industrial, hilly) and cluster type (core, peripheral). A large language model (LLM) orchestrated causal discovery pipeline adapts algorithm selection to local data conditions across 57 city-cluster units. The library reveals systematic variation. Core demand is driven by activity access and transit proximity, while peripheral demand responds to built form, with city-type-specific patterns supporting transferable siting templates. A planning tool built on the library scores candidate sites, calibrates infrastructure recommendations to local demographics, and generates practitioner-ready reports. In Heilbronn, Germany, two hub sites informed by the framework's causal evidence are currently under construction, illustrating how the outputs can support real-world siting decisions.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6993492`

- title: Vision Language Action Models for Autonomous Driving and 3D Environment: Towards Agentic Intelligence, Frameworks, Benchmarks, and Open Challenges
- authors: Gulraiz Khan, Waqas Ahmed, K.Y. Wertheim, Kevin Pimbblet, Muhammad Khalid
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-25
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6993492
- keyword hits: agentic, large language model, large language models

### abstract

Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have attracted significant attention in recent years due to their broad applicability across fields, including healthcare, autonomous vehicles (AVs), robotics, 3D reconstruction, and human-computer interaction. The usefulness of these models has enabled the development of Vision–Language–Action (VLA) models. These models are not only capable of perceiving and analyzing different types of inputs (visual data and natural language), but also of processing multimodal information and formulating responses and actions based on reasoning. Researchers across domains proactively embrace VLAs to advance their respective fields, particularly in autonomous vehicles using 3D space. VLA models have enabled autonomous vehicles to understand directions, navigate through complicated traffic scenarios, and formulate independent driving actions. However, the current literature in both domains (3D environment and autonomous vehicles) remains scattered yet growing rapidly. To the best of our knowledge, this paper presents the first unified and comprehensive survey of VLA systems for autonomous vehicles and 3D reconstruction. We reviewed around 60 systems and, in the end, discussed open challenges and potential future directions for both applications. This survey provides a comprehensive reference guide for developing context-aware VLA systems with reasoning and understanding in autonomous driving ecosystems.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6895958`

- title: Reconciliation-Related Fintech Complaints Receive Monetary Relief at Nearly Three Times the Rate of Other Payment Complaints, 2021-2025
- authors: Ignacio Berardi
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-25
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6895958
- keyword hits: large language model, prompting

### abstract

This study analyzes 97,028 consumer complaints filed with the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) between January 2021 and December 2025 against eleven major US consumer fintechs, classifying each complaint by whether it describes a pattern consistent with a reconciliation failure. Reconciliation is defined as the process of confirming that two or more records of the same money movement agree; a reconciliation failure occurs when those records diverge and the discrepancy produces a consumer-visible outcome such as a transfer that never arrives, a duplicate charge that is not reversed, or a refund that does not post. The central finding is that reconciliation-related complaints close with CFPB-recorded monetary relief at 9.96%, compared to 3.51% for all other fintech payment complaints in the dataset, a gap of nearly three times. Monetary relief in CFPB terminology means the company paid the consumer to resolve the dispute: a refund, restitution payment, or fee reversal. It is the strongest resolution outcome recorded by the CFPB and reflects a company determination, made under regulatory review, that the consumer was owed money. The near-3x gap holds in direction across all four product categories in the dataset, with reconciliation-related complaints receiving monetary relief at higher rates than non-reconciliation complaints in every category, and relief ratios ranging from 1.4x to 4.0x depending on product type. In 2021 through 2024, prior to a regulatory event that inflated 2025 complaint volume approximately sevenfold, reconciliation-related complaints represented between 12.95% and 15.64% of all fintech payment complaints annually, with a pooled four-year share of 14.39%, or roughly one in seven. The 2025 data are included in full for transparency and excluded from prevalence estimates; the rationale is detailed in Section 5. Classification was performed by a reasoning-capable large language model using chain-ofthought prompting. Validation against 80 hand-labeled complaints produced accuracy of 88.75% (95% CI: 80.0%-94.0%), precision of 89.19% (95% CI: 75.3%-95.7%), recall of 86.84% (95% CI: 72.7%-94.2%), specificity of 90.48% (95% CI: 77.9%-96.2%), and F1 of 88.00%. All confidence intervals use the Wilson method (Wilson, 1927). Precision and recall are balanced, indicating the classifier neither systematically over-flags nor under-flags. The full classified dataset, validation set, and CFPB source query are published for independent audit.

## uid: `arxiv:2606.26901v1`

- title: SamaVaani: Auditing and Debiasing Multilingual Clinical ASR for Indian Languages
- authors: Subham Kumar, Prakrithi Shivaprakash, Abhishek Manoharan, Astut Kurariya, Diptadhi Mukherjee, Prabhat Chand, Pratima Murthy, Koustav Rudra
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-25
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26901v1
- keyword hits: fine-tuning, gemini

### abstract

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) is increasingly used to document clinical encounters, yet its reliability in multilingual and demographically diverse Indian healthcare context remains largely unknown. In this study, we first conduct the systematic audit of ASR performance on real-world psychiatric interview data spanning Kannada, Hindi and Indian English, comparing eight state-of-the-art models including IndicWhisper, WhisperLargeV3, Sarvam, GoogleS2T, Gemma3n, OmniLingual, Vaani, and Gemini. Our results reveal substantial variability across models and languages, with some systems performing competitively in Indian English but failing in regional speech. We further fine-tune two of the best performing opensource models, i.e., Gemma3n and OmniLingual, using various methods. With this, we uncover systematic performance gaps tied to speaker role and gender, raising concerns about equitable deployment in clinical settings, which are further mitigated by fairness-aware fine-tuning. To this end, we propose SamaVaani, a unified debiasing technique that simultaneously improves ASR performance and improves fairness across demographic groups.
