- title: AI Availability: Extending Mental and Physical Availability into Algorithmic Retrieval
- authors: Pablo Ulpiano Gonzalez Castro
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6659000
- keyword hits: agentic, generative ai

### abstract

Generative AI is collapsing brand consideration sets, in many categories, into a single recommendation returned against paraphrased intent-fundamentally altering how brands enter or fail to enter consumer choice. The substrate introduces a paradox: the same mechanism that amplifies incumbent advantage through training-corpus prominence also creates the only systematic conditions under which that advantage is disrupted. This article extends the Ehrenberg-Bass framework by introducing AI availability-the propensity of a brand to be surfaced by generative AI against paraphrased consumer intent-as a third dimension of retrieval, and formalises the paradox that defines its operation. A unifying proposition (P0) specifies the mechanism: training-corpus prominence interacting with single-recommendation output. Two outcome propositions specify reinforcement of incumbent hierarchies in the modal case (P1) and selective suspension under jointly-met conditionsutilitarian category, low identity load, single-recommendation interface, AI-trusting consumer (P2). Two moderators (identity load; brand familiarity) bound the conditions (P3, P4). The framework operates on existing brand-equity assets in the present configuration, with pathways to partial displacement specified for agentic configurations. An experiment (n = 208) demonstrates a 25.4 percentage-point choice shift consistent with the suspension prediction. Three operational tools translate the framework into brand-portfolio practice.

- title: From Reporting to Inference: Active LP Decision-Making in Private Markets
- authors: Kristine Miranda
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6721599
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Private markets now exceed $24 trillion in assets under management, yet the tools used to monitor them remain constrained by fragmented data, reporting lag, and the smoothing effects of appraisal-based valuation. The result is a structural gap between reported NAVs and underlying economic reality. That gap has gained immediate economic significance with the explosive growth of private market secondaries: annual secondary transactions reached $240 billion in 2025, a sixfold increase over the previous decade, with $166 billion flowing into dedicated secondaries funds in the same year. Secondary pricing turns directly on how much reported valuations should be trusted, making the inference gap a transactional one, not merely an analytical one. This paper argues that private market monitoring is fundamentally an inference problem, not a measurement problem. Reported NAVs are one observation among several about a latent fund value, and the LP's task is to update beliefs as new evidence arrives. Drawing on conversations with allocators across family offices and institutions, the paper proposes three evidence channels for that inference: a credibility weight on reported NAVs, a structural prior on stress sensitivity derived from observable fund characteristics, and a non-transactional channel that extracts signal from the timing and language of disclosure. The framework is positioned within the Bayesian state-space tradition (Geltner; Brown, Ghysels, and Gredil) but designed to be tractable at scale through agentic systems that current language models make practical. What was once feasible only for the largest institutional investors, multi-signal, longitudinal monitoring of private portfolios, is now within reach of a much broader range of LPs. The empirical contribution, whether narrative signals carry information that transaction-anchored frameworks miss during silent-stress periods, is hypothesized here and tested in a companion paper. The goal is not to predict outcomes or replace investment judgment. It is to help investors ask better questions, sooner.

- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-06
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6634778

### abstract

The convergence of decentralised computing paradigms, autonomous artificial intelligence (AI) agents, and distributed ledger technologies is reshaping the cybersecurity landscape in profound and previously unanticipated ways. This systematic literature review synthesises 140 peer-reviewed studies published between 2017 and 2026 to provide a comprehensive examination of security, privacy, and trust in agentic and decentralised AI systems. Following PRISMA 2020 guidelines, the review spans eight thematic pillars: foundations of decentralised and agentic AI architectures; threat models in distributed and autonomous settings; privacy-preserving learning mechanisms; cryptographic foundations; blockchain and distributed ledger integration; IoT and edge AI security; Byzantine resilience and trust; and open research challenges. The findings reveal that whilst federated learning (FL) substantially reduces raw-data exposure, gradient-level attacks, model poisoning, Byzantine failures, and the privacy-utility trade-off remain critical unsolved problems. The review identifies a pressing need for standardised benchmarks, formal threat models for long-horizon autonomous agents, and deeper integration of post-quantum cryptographic primitives.

### abstract

Current end-to-end multi-object tracking (MOT) methods face three primary bottlenecks: the intrinsic mismatch between local supervised signals and global trajectory metrics, rigid predefined module coordination, and the stability-plasticity dilemma caused by traditional full-parameter fine-tuning. To address these limitations, we propose Query-MARFT, a novel Query-Guided Multi-Agent Reinforcement Fine-Tuning framework. We reconceptualize the evolution of track queries as a Flexible Markov Game, decoupling the trajectory lifecycle into four role-specific agents: Detection Focus, Association Modulation, Temporal Update, and Trajectory Correction. To accommodate diverse visual dynamics, these agents collaborate dynamically via a scene-adaptive Directed Acyclic Graph topology. Furthermore, to overcome the credit assignment challenge in multi-agent reinforcement learning, we introduce a hierarchical reward mechanism optimized via Group Relative Policy Optimization. By integrating a Low-Rank Adaptation based parameter-efficient fine-tuning strategy with a two-stage training pipeline, Query-MARFT achieves an optimal balance between acquiring task-specific trajectory reasoning and retaining pre-trained zero-shot representations. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art HOTA scores of 74.1 and 65.3 on the highly challenging DanceTrack and MOT20 benchmarks, respectively. It substantially reduces identity switches while exhibiting exceptional trajectory continuity and robust cross-domain generalization. Code is available at https://github.com/JoshuaWenHIT/Query-MARFT

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6674761`

- title: The Agentic 3 C's Framework A Reasoning-Layer Risk Governance Model for Agentic AI in Financial Services
- authors: Maureen Doyle-Spare
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-07
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6674761
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

A new failure mode is emerging in agentic AI deployments: systems executing correctly against meaning that no human explicitly approved or authorized. The failure does not originate in data quality, model accuracy, or workflow design. It originates at the reasoning-layer, where agentic systems resolve context across enterprise platforms before execution. Existing governance frameworks do not yet explicitly govern how meaning is resolved at this layer prior to execution. This paper introduces the Agentic 3 C’s Framework as the operating principles required at the reasoning-layer to enable safe, trusted agentic AI at scale in financial services. The framework establishes Context, Control, and Coordination as the three conditions under which an agentic system’s reasoning can be considered governable. Context establishes the authoritative meaning the system is permitted to resolve against. Control constrains how the system is permitted to act on that meaning. Coordination governs how meaning, authority, and execution propagate across multiple agents and enterprise systems. The 3 C’s are positioned as the foundational operating principles that the author’s prior work implicitly relied upon. SSRN Working Paper No. 6459612 introduced Agentic Workflow Drift and Agentic Workflow Subversion as failures that occur when these principles are not enforced. SSRN Working Paper No. 6531238 introduced the Semantic Deviation Index as the runtime measurement standard that operates within Control. This paper formalizes the 3 C’s as the framework those constructs sit within, and positions a forthcoming companion paper on the Semantic Control Plane as the runtime enforcement architecture that operationalizes the framework at scale. The paper demonstrates that reasoning-layer risk does not replace existing risk categories. It conditions them. Each existing category becomes dependent on whether reasoning occurred against authorized meaning before execution. The framework can be mapped against the principal regulatory and supervisory frameworks under which agentic AI in financial services is governed, including the NIST AI Risk Management Framework, the European Union Artificial Intelligence Act, and the Cyber Risk Institute Financial Services AI Risk Management Framework, while making explicit where those frameworks do not yet specify how the reasoning layer itself should be governed. Drawing on practitioner expertise in banking controls, model risk governance, and enterprise architecture, this paper is submitted as original practitioner-led research that defines the conditions under which agentic execution becomes governable in regulated financial services environments.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6727868`

- title: Bayesian Optimized VMD-MPE-CEEMDAN for Long-term Photovoltaic Power Prediction
- authors: junxiong zhan, chenyu qi, yaru zhang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-07
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6727868
- keyword hits: transformer model

### abstract

At present, photovoltaic power forecasting faces several critical limitations, including distorted selection of similar days, poor signal decomposition quality, excessive training time of prediction models, and high susceptibility to local optima, which severely restrict the improvement of forecasting accuracy. In this study, a novel hybrid photovoltaic power forecasting model integrating a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)-transformer is proposed, which combines comprehensive distance-based similar-day selection and Bayesian-optimized VMD-MPE-CEEMDAN signal decomposition. First, the Pearson correlation coefficient (PCC) is adopted to screen key meteorological factors. Second, a comprehensive similar day selection method is established by the weighted coupling of six indicators, namely, the spearman correlation coefficient, mahalanobis distance, grey relational degree, dynamic time warping (DTW), euclidean distance and cosine similarity, to determine the historical similar days corresponding to the target forecasting day. Subsequently, variational mode decomposition (VMD), complete ensemble empirical mode decomposition with adaptive noise (CEEMDAN), and the Bayesian-optimized hybrid decomposition strategy are jointly applied to decompose the historical power generation series, thereby constructing high-quality training datasets. Finally, a combined forecasting framework based on deep learning (DL) algorithms is established to implement model training, prediction and quantitative evaluation of photovoltaic output. The experimental results demonstrate that, compared with other hybrid forecasting models, the proposed CNN-Transformer model with comprehensive similar day matching and Bayesian-optimized VMD-MPE-CEEMDAN decomposition can effectively capture the mutation characteristics of photovoltaic power series and achieve superior forecasting performance. This research provides a reliable technical reference for the optimal dispatch and operation management of photovoltaic power stations.
