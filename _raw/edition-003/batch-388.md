- title: A Transformer-Based Autoencoder Framework for State of Health Estimation of Lithium-Ion Batteries Using Partial Charging Segments
- authors: Longshen Chen, Menggen Zhu, weiwu yan, Peng Wang, Chao Wang, Jia Luo, Xi Zhang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6853889
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Accurate state estimation of battery energy storage systems is crucial for improving battery utilization and prolonging service life. However, in practical applications, state-of-health (SOH) estimation is often hindered by fragmented charging data and limited labeled samples. To address these issues, this study develops a novel SOH estimation framework based on a Transformer autoencoder that operates on incomplete charging segments. A hybrid data augmentation strategy, incorporating noise injection, random masking, and segment truncation, is introduced to simulate realistic data imperfections and enhance model robustness. The Transformer-based autoencoder is designed to capture long-range dependencies along the voltage axis and extract degradation-relevant latent representations. Furthermore, a two-stage learning strategy consisting of self-supervised pre-training and subsequent fine-tuning is adopted to effectively exploit abundant unlabeled data alongside limited labeled samples. Experimental results on multiple lithium-ion battery datasets demonstrate that the proposed framework achieves accurate and stable SOH estimation under partial charging conditions, highlighting its potential for real-world battery management applications.​

- title: Stereo lightfield sensing for Vehicle-Infrastructure Cooperative Perception in CCAM: challenges and opportunities
- authors: Sergi Mercadé, Josep Brugués, Romain Guesdon, Pablo Martín, Ricardo Oliva, Leonel Toledo, Aleksandar Jevtić, Jesús Alonso
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6854500
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

This paper evaluates Stereo Lightfield (STLF) cameras as a cost-effective alternative to LiDAR for vehicle-infrastructure Cooperative Perception (CP). Leveraging STLF's ability to merge rich visual context with dense depth-sensing, we propose a distributed architecture featuring vehicle-mounted STLF arrays, infrastructure cameras, and spatial compression for V2X data exchange.Our evaluation spans theoretical validation to real-world deployment. Simulations confirm that STLF-generated point clouds integrate seamlessly with state-of-the-art 3D object detection and cooperative tracking models. Furthermore, dynamic spatial compression effectively mitigates V2X bandwidth bottlenecks. Real-world tests, comparing standalone STLF nodes against on-board LiDAR, demonstrate 3D perception capabilities with relative depth errors between 5% and 13%.However, deploying a fully cooperative multi-node system exposed critical hardware and algorithmic challenges, including domain shifts in infrastructure perception, USB bandwidth limits for multi-camera arrays, and severe geometric stitching distortions preventing reliable multi-node calibration. By highlighting these empirical roadblocks alongside STLF's theoretical promise, this work outlines a technical roadmap targeting dedicated edge processing, advanced non-planar stitching, and domain-specific fine-tuning to mature STLF into a viable foundational technology for future collaborative automotive safety systems.

- title: When Users Learn to ”Speak AI”:,Prompting as Communicative Imbrication in Human-Machine Communication
- authors: Jiayao Li, Jiayi Yang, Liwen Zhang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6850489
- keyword hits: generative ai, prompting

### abstract

This study examines how users engage with generative AI chatbots through everyday prompting practices and how these interactions shape communicative patterns as well as perceptions of agency. Drawing on semi-structured interviews and prompt-elicitation sessions, this study provides a qualitative, practice-oriented account of human-AI interaction (HAII) in context. The findings show that prompting functions as an iterative communicative process in which users refine language, structure requests, and respond to system outputs through cycles of adjustment and repair. Participants also calibrate their interaction based on perceived model capability, and modify expectations, provide feedback, and adapt strategies accordingly. Across these processes, agency is experienced as distributed, with users treating AI as a supportive but limited collaborator while retaining responsibility for evaluation and control. Situated within Human-Machine Communication (HMC), the study contributes empirical insight into how the co-construction of meaning and agency unfolds in everyday interaction and uses the term ”communicative imbrication” as an analytical shorthand to describe how human and machine contributions become interwoven across successive prompting exchanges.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6854330`

- title: A Comprehensive Artificial Intelligence Approach for a Spatio-Temporal PM2.5 Forecasting System in the Non-Attainment Cities of Rajasthan
- authors: Abhijeet Mondal, Rajyalakshmi Garaga, Irwindeep Singh
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-05-30
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6854330
- keyword hits: transformer model

### abstract

Rajasthan is home to several designated non-attainment cities where ambient air quality standards are persistently violated by the combined effects of desert dust, vehicular emissions, and industrial activity. The study examines the spatiotemporal variability of air pollutants (PM2.5, PM10, NO, NO2, NOx, SO2, CO, and O3), alongside meteorological parameters (RH, WS, and WD) across five non-attainment cities: Alwar, Jaipur, Jodhpur, Kota, and Udaipur, using CPCB data from 2018 to 2024. AQI trend analysis showed that winter months had the worst deterioration across all cities, with PM consistently the predominant contributor. Kendall rank correlation confirmed strong associations between PM fractions and AQI, while WS and RH were identified as key meteorological factors influencing pollutant concentrations. Two deep learning models (LSTM and Transformer) and two machine learning models (Random Forest and XGBoost) were trained and evaluated, with city-specific best-fit models selected based on performance metrics. The Transformer model performed best for Alwar, and the LSTM model performed best for Jaipur, Jodhpur, and Kota, while the RF model performed best for Udaipur. SHAP analysis provided clear feature attribution for each model, confirming that PM fractions are the most influential across all cities. Predictive modelling was used to validate PM2.5 based on training data. Best-fit models were subsequently deployed to forecast independent year-long (2025) PM2.5 concentrations. This study presents the first comprehensive approach to forecast year-round PM2.5 levels in non-attainment cities in Rajasthan, supporting air quality monitoring and forecasting in urban environments in India.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6862084`

- title: Cross-condition and cross-chemistry battery prognostics with domain-adversarial and variance-regularized hierarchical transformers
- authors: Liu Teng, Wei Li, Zhiqiang Li, Weiwei Huo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6862084
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Accurate remaining useful life (RUL) prediction of lithium-ion batteries under varying operating conditions and diverse chemistries remains challenging due to complex distribution shifts in degradation patterns. This paper proposes a unified prognostic framework integrating hierarchical transformers with domain-adversarial training and variance regularization for zero-shot cross-condition and cross-chemistry battery capacity trajectory forecasting. The framework encodes SOC-aligned within-cycle measurements into compact cycle representations via a lower-level transformer with attention pooling, then captures long-range degradation dynamics through an upper-level transformer to predict future capacity. Domain-adversarial learning with a gradient reversal layer suppresses domain-specific shortcuts, enabling zero-shot transfer across unseen datasets and chemistries without fine-tuning. Variance-regularized risk minimization penalizes loss dispersion across condition-chemistry subgroups to improve worst-case performance. The model is evaluated on an aggregated multi-chemistry dataset (LCO, NCA, NCM, blended cathode, sodium-ion) under -5 °C to 60 °C and 0.2C to 6.0C. Six benchmark protocols with progressively relaxed split keys systematically assess zero-shot generalization across cells, discharge rates, operating conditions, and chemistry-domain holdouts. Results show trajectory-level MAPE of 1.02%–8.44% and end-of-life MAPE of 5.01%–9.69% under zero-shot settings, with longer observation prefixes consistently improving accuracy. Ablation studies confirm that removing hierarchical encoding, domain-adversarial training, attention pooling, or variance regularization substantially increases errors under strong domain shifts, validating their complementary synergy for robust zero-shot battery prognostics.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6862104`

- title: Health status prediction of key components of traction motors based on BiLSTM-Transformer
- authors: Yumei Liu, Huanjing Zeng, Jingzhuo Liu, Ming Liu, Zongyao Liu
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6862104
- keyword hits: transformer model

### abstract

This paper provides effective technical support for the implementation of health state prediction functionality in traction motor health management systems through research on bearing health state prediction methods. Firstly, 16 time-domain features and 12 frequency-domain features of bearing vibration signals were extracted, and a comprehensive evaluation index encompassing correlation, monotonicity, and robustness was constructed. The weights of each evaluation index were then determined using the entropy weight method. Secondly, through comprehensive evaluation, the top 15 features with the highest scores were selected as degradation-sensitive features. The selected degradation features were then fused using a Self-Organizing Map network, the minimum quantization error was calculated, and the features were processed using the Cumulative Sum Control Chart method to construct a health state quantification index which is Health Indicator (HI). HI was divided into five health state levels: healthy, sub-healthy, qualified, warning, and fault, and a health state prediction model based on Bidirectional Long Short-Term Memory-Transformer (BiLSTM-Transformer) was further constructed. Finally, to verify the effectiveness of the model, a mixed training set was constructed using HI data from different models of bearings under various working conditions in the XJTU-SY bearing dataset. The HI curves of bearings not involved in the training were predicted. The experimental results showed that the constructed model exhibited good generalization ability and robustness. Furthermore, using the same data, the good performance of the constructed model in health state prediction was further verified by comparing it with Fuzzy Neural Network, Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), Bidirectional LSTM (BiLSTM), and Transformer models.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6805139`

- title: Agentic AI in Derivatives Markets: Counterparties, Risk, and the Regulatory Gap
- authors: Ligia Catherine Arias-Barrera
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6805139
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Agentic artificial intelligence systems, characterised by goal-directed, multi-step, adaptive architectures that integrate data perception, strategic reasoning, and autonomous execution, have moved beyond the threshold of theoretical concern and now function as de facto participants in over-the-counter (OTC) derivatives markets. The legal frameworks governing that market, spanning market participant law, risk regulation, and contractual infrastructure, were built on an assumption that has silently expired: that every consequential market decision is made by a human or corporate principal capable of bearing rights, giving consent, and answering for its conduct. The resulting void is tripartite, encompassing legal capacity doctrine, liability allocation, and systemic risk governance, and each component is mapped systematically in this article. In its place, this article proposes a functional-accountability framework organised around the concept of accountability anchoring: the assignment of legal and regulatory responsibility to identifiable human or institutional principals at each decisional layer of the agentic architecture, calibrated to the degree of autonomy exercised at that layer. The analysis draws on the technical architecture of agentic finance developed by Gong, 1 the empirical evidence on autonomous agents in decentralised finance offered by Ante, 2 and the sociological theory of science and technology advanced by Cotgrove 3 and Buttel. 4 The sociological frame illuminates why the regulatory gap exists as a structural function of institutional deference toward claims of technical authority, and what kind of governance response is adequate to it.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6867798`

- title: Multi-Scale Factor-Integrated BA-LSTM Model for Medium- to Long-Term Runoff Forecasting Across Multiple Basins
- authors: Jinggang Chu, Lei Ye, Wenyu Ouyang, Xiangyi Le, Lei Zhang, Rijie Huang
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-06-02
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6867798
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Against the backdrop of climate change, medium- to long-term runoff forecasting is crucial for water resources management. While deep learning models, particularly Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) networks, have shown substantial promise in unified multi-basin modeling, they struggle to capture the spatially heterogeneous impacts of global- and astronomical-scale teleconnection factors. To address this gap, we propose the Basin-Aware Long Short-Term Memory LSTM (BA-LSTM), which introduces a dynamic feature modulation module based on static basin properties. For the first time, global and astronomical factors are explicitly incorporated into a unified multi-basin modeling framework, equipped with learnable basin-aware weights that modulate the heterogeneous sensitivities of different basins to identical macroscopic forcings. Tested across 669 basins globally, the BA-LSTM achieved a median testing Nash-Sutcliffe Efficiency (NSE) of 0.776, outperforming both local basin-by-basin models with an NSE of 0.706 and traditional global LSTM architectures with an NSE of 0.698, representing improvements of 9.9% and 11.2%, respectively. Spatially, the proposed framework demonstrated exceptional robustness across diverse continental hydro-climatic regimes, effectively mitigating the regional predictive biases inherent in traditional modeling. Furthermore, we demonstrated the model’s strong cross-basin generalization through transfer learning in data-scarce regions of China, specifically the Songhua River and Wu River basins, where a fine-tuning strategy leveraging global pre-trained weights significantly improved predictive reliability over local benchmarks by achieving testing NSE values of 0.791 and 0.874, respectively. Finally, an Integrated Gradients (IG) analysis was conducted to interpret the internal model sensitivity, revealing that basin-scale factors dominate, with precipitation contributing approximately 50% of the relative sensitivity, while explicit multi-scale varying feature importance within the trained BA-LSTM is captured. Our findings provide a robust and interpretable framework for integrating multi-scale forcings into large-sample hydrological modeling.
