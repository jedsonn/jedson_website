## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.6900960`

- title: The Workforce Operating System: A Unified Architecture for Agentic Workforce Management and Enterprise Value
- authors: Garrett Walker
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.6900960
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Every major operating domain in the modern enterprise has a unified system of record and control. Finance has its financial operating model, supply chain its sales-and-operations planning, sales its revenue architecture, and technology its enterprise architecture. The workforce-simultaneously the largest cost on the income statement and the largest unpulled lever of value creation on the balance sheet-has none. This paper introduces the Workforce Operating System (WOS): a unified architecture that prices, deploys, measures, and attributes workforce capacity to enterprise value with the same rigor that finance applies to capital. WOS is offered not as a new idea but as the next chapter of a measurement lineage that began with the human-capital scorecard work of the early 2000s and has matured, across two decades of strategic human-resource scholarship, to the point where the next advance is no longer analytic but architectural. The paper advances a single organizing principle-the Systems Law: artificial intelligence amplifies whatever system it inherits, chaos or value-and derives from it the sequencing requirement that an operating system must precede agentic deployment, not accompany it. It then asks why a system the field has worked toward for over twenty-five years becomes buildable only now, and locates the answer in the disciplinary seam between human-resource architecture and financial operation. Its central contribution is the attribution chain, the mechanism by which a unit of human capability is priced, deployed, measured, and traced to earnings. The paper closes by locating the natural proving ground for that mechanism in private-capital value creation, a context the literature, centered on the corporate enterprise, has not yet taken up.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7032061`

- title: Copula-Based Scenario Generation from Probabilistic PV Forecasts for Stochastic Energy Management Systems
- authors: Francesco  Maria Tiburtini, Najmeh Bazmohammadi, Mashid Javidsharifi, Francesco Lo Franco, Juan  C. Vasquez, Mattia Ricco
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7032061
- keyword hits: transformer model

### abstract

The growing penetration of distributed photovoltaic (PV) generation coupled with battery energy storage systems (BESS) requires accurate representation of PV uncertainty for reliable and effective energy management. Although probabilistic forecasting models provide calibrated marginal distributions, generating scenarios by sampling each future time step independently ignores the strong inter-temporal correlation of PV generation, producing unrealistic trajectories that reduce the operational value of stochastic energy management systems (EMS). This paper proposes a day-ahead PV scenario generation framework combining a Temporal Fusion Transformer model with a Gaussian copula to produce temporally coherent scenarios based on day-specific meteorological forecasts. The Gaussian copula restores the temporal dependence over the 24-hours horizon, and finally a backward reduction algorithm yields a compact scenario set for stochastic optimization. The framework is validated on real-world data retrieved from four PV plants in southern Italy. Scenario quality assessment confirm substantial improvement in temporal coherence over independent sampling. Operational value is evaluated through a two-stage stochastic EMS for BESS dispatch, yielding up to 20.2% cost reduction and statistically significant improvements over deterministic and stochastic benchmark strategies. The achieved results establish a causal chain linking forecast calibration, scenario quality, and operational performance, highlighting that the copula’s effectiveness is conditional on the calibration of the generated marginal distributions.

- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7032104

### abstract

With the rise of AI agents, human–agent collaboration has become a key driver of green innovation in manufacturing. This study examines how employee-led, technology-led, and co-led collaboration influence corporate green innovation from the perspective of authority allocation. Survey evidence from Chinese high-tech manufacturing firms shows that all three collaboration modes promote green innovation, with co-led collaboration producing the strongest effect. ## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7037863`

- title: An Agentic AI Educational Framework Integrating Self-Regulated Learning and Learning Analytics
- authors: Daniel Chang, Michael Pin-Chuan Lin, Jing-Yuan Huang, Jeeho Ryoo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7037863
- keyword hits: agentic, generative artificial intelligence

### abstract

The emergence of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) tools in higher education has outpaced theoretical frameworks for understanding how these technologies can support self-regulated learning (SRL). GenAI tools, despite their growing popularity, are reactive systems that cannot perceive learner states, maintain longitudinal models, or proactively intervene. Conventional learning analytics (LA) approaches face a parallel limitation. For example, behavioural traces are recorded without reference to the content being processed, producing what Winne (2023) termed “empty” events. Agentic AI, on the other hand, can be distinguished from GenAI by goal-directed autonomy, persistent learner modeling, tool use, and proactive intervention. This conceptual paper proposes the Agentic-SRL Interface (ASRL-I), a conceptual framework integrating three SRL traditions (Winne’s COPES/SMART, Zimmerman’s cyclical model, and Pintrich’s four-phase model) with contextually enriched LA and agentic AI capabilities. Four theoretical propositions are advanced: (1) agentic AI can transform behavioural traces into content-contextualized SRL signals; (2) interventions can be phase-congruent with Zimmerman's regulatory cycle; (3) four information properties mediate metacognitive awareness and operationalize calibrated scaffolding; (4) motivational states can be modeled longitudinally to distinguish trait from situational dynamics. Theoretical, design, and pedagogical implications are discussed, alongside future empirical directions for testing the framework’s propositions.

## uid: `doi:10.2139/ssrn.7038095`

- title: A Mask-Enhanced Deep Learning Framework for Robust State-of-Charge Estimation of Lithium-Ion Batteries under Distribution Shifts
- authors: Liang Ma, Shiming Tian, Hanjiang Ma, wanzhou sun, Jinpeng Tian, Chunsheng Hu, Shanshan Guo, Qinghua Guo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.7038095
- keyword hits: fine-tuning

### abstract

Accurate state-of-charge (SOC) estimation is indispensable for the reliable operation of lithium-ion battery management systems. Nevertheless, the performance of existing deep learning models is often compromised when deployed under practical operating conditions, where temperature variations, unseen driving patterns, sensor faults, and battery-to-battery discrepancies induce significant distribution shifts. To address this challenge, this study develops a mask-enhanced deep learning framework for robust SOC estimation. A temporal masking strategy is incorporated into an LSTM-based network to randomly mask a subset of input observations during training. This strategy encourages the model to learn robust battery representations that generalize effectively to unseen operating conditions without requiring additional retraining or fine-tuning. Extensive evaluations are conducted under unknown temperatures, unseen drive cycles, sensor anomalies, varying input sequence lengths, and different battery chemistries. The proposed framework consistently outperforms conventional machine learning and deep learning methods across all evaluation scenarios. Under unknown-temperature conditions, the root mean square error (RMSE) and mean absolute error (MAE) are reduced from 18.43% and 16.20% to 10.13% and 9.13%, respectively, while under an unseen driving cycle they further decrease from 30.43% and 24.60% to 7.16% and 5.82%, respectively. Moreover, the proposed framework demonstrates strong transferability across different battery types and maintains stable estimation performance under sensor perturbations, highlighting its robustness to practical deployment challenges. Overall, this work provides a simple yet effective solution for reliable data-driven SOC estimation and facilitates the practical deployment of trustworthy battery management systems under complex real-world operating conditions.

## uid: `arxiv:2607.01421v1`

- title: Risk Architecture for AI-Native Engineering Teams: An Organizational Framework for Agentic System Governance
- authors: Laxmipriya Ganesh Iyer
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01421v1
- keyword hits: agentic

### abstract

Engineering management research has produced mature frameworks for software risk: ownership by feature, escalation by severity, and assurance by test coverage. These frameworks implicitly assume deterministic behavior, discrete and auditable change events, and clear component-to-owner mappings. Teams that build and operate agentic AI systems violate all three assumptions at once: outputs are probabilistic, systems take autonomous multi-step actions, and the risk surface mutates silently between deployments. Existing AI risk literature addresses this from above (policy frameworks such as the NIST AI RMF and ISO/IEC 42001) or below (threat taxonomies such as OWASP's agentic AI guidance), but not at the layer where an engineering manager (EM) operates: roles, decision rights, and escalation structures. This paper contributes (i) a seven-dimension profile distinguishing pure software-engineering, hybrid, and AI-native teams; (ii) a six-cluster failure-mode taxonomy including a previously unarticulated cluster, dependency-boundary determinism mismatch; and (iii) a synthetic framework-adequacy methodology scoring how well each profile's risk architecture detects, contains, and escalates a defined scenario set. Because the object of study is framework adequacy rather than human behavior, the evaluation yields derived rather than observed coverage claims. Coverage degrades as teams move from pure software engineering to AI-native operation, monotonically in the median and abruptly in the count of uncovered, high-consequence failures appearing only at the AI-native step. The degradation concentrates in specific failure-mode categories, and the most severe, least-covered failures arise not inside AI-native teams but at the organizational boundary where their probabilistic outputs are consumed by determinism-assuming dependencies.

## uid: `arxiv:2607.01023v1`

- title: Evidence-Supported Credit Risk Report Generation Using News-Centric Financial Knowledge Graphs
- authors: Rocio Jimenez-Villen, Ziwei Xu, Ying Chen, Oscar Araque, Ryutaro Ichise
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01023v1
- keyword hits: in-context learning

### abstract

Financial markets evolve in response to real-world events reported in news, yet these drivers often remain implicit in text. To better explain market dynamics, event-market relations must be explicitly modeled through factual, company-centric, and environment-aware knowledge graphs. We present FinKG-News, a framework that automatically constructs such graphs by extracting news events as anchors linked to companies. Using FinKG-News as grounded evidence that integrates events, news, and company data, we develop an in-context learning architecture for credit risk report generation across three core financial dimensions. Automatic and human evaluations show that automated hallucination detection and quality assessment remain unreliable, making expert judgment indispensable. Our approach consistently outperforms baselines, improving quality by 19%-34% while reducing hallucinations. The source code and project resources are publicly available at: https://github.com/ichise-laboratory/FINKG-news.

- title: CHARLIE: An On-Premise Multi-Agent Retrieval-Augmented Generation System for Evidential Reasoning in Forensic Science
- authors: Leandro D. Carneiro, Andre L. S. Meirelles, Juliano de A. Gomes, Rafael C. A. Cabral
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2026-07-01
- source: arXiv
- link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05428v1
- keyword hits: retrieval-augmented

### abstract

We present Charlie, an on-premise multi-agent Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system for structured evidential processing in digital forensic environments. Contemporary forensic workflows must handle large volumes of heterogeneous and unstructured documents under strict requirements of traceability, confidentiality, and legal compliance. Charlie addresses this challenge through a controlled agent architecture that combines local retrieval, task decomposition, structured memory, and verification mechanisms. Unlike cloud-based systems, it operates entirely within institutional infrastructure, preserving data sovereignty and evidential integrity. We describe the systems architecture, including its transition from classical RAG to agent-based orchestration, and demonstrate its application in real-world forensic scenarios. Case studies show that Charlie enables scalable multi-document data extraction and supports longitudinal forensic intelligence generation while maintaining traceability and auditability. Our results indicate that agent-orchestrated, on-premise RAG architectures can effectively support evidential workflows without compromising legal and institutional constraints. Charlie provides a practical and reproducible blueprint for deploying AI systems in high-stakes forensic environments. This manuscript is an archival version of a paper presented at the RELAF 2026 Workshop.
