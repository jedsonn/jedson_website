- title: Modelling Custom: Large Language Models and Customary International Law between Scarcity to Abundance
- authors: Tamar Megiddo
- affiliations: not stated
- posted: 2025-03-28
- source: SSRN
- link: https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.5115997
- keyword hits: large language model, large language models

### abstract

The identification of customary international law has long been shaped by a narrative of scarcity with respect to evidence, access and analytical capacity. This scarcity has contributed, among other things, to a persistent global North bias in the identification and formation of custom. However, recent legal and technological developments have ushered in an era of abundance, marked by both unprecedented access to data and powerful tools for its analysis. Extensive information on states’ actions and legal positions—across a broader range of jurisdictions, languages and formats—is now accessible, and large-scale computational analysis is increasingly feasible. This Article explores the implications of these developments for the study of customary international law. It argues that a data-driven approach, leveraging large language models to analyze state practice and opinio juris from carefully constructed datasets that account for diversity, offers significant promise for overcoming traditional challenges in the identification of custom, and may transform how customary norms are identified and studied.
